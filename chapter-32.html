📖 THE EXAM MOMENT

You have written three strong paragraphs. Your context was clear (Ch. 13–18). Your argument was developed (Ch. 19–24). Your solution, request, or call to action was precise (Ch. 26–30). You deployed conditional and subjunctive structures with confidence (Ch. 31). You glance at your word count — you are at approximately 190 words. Good.

Now you face a moment that separates experienced letter writers from beginners.

The beginner thinks: "I've said everything. Time for the closing formula." And they jump directly from their last argument sentence into "Veuillez agréer, Madame, Monsieur, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées."

The experienced writer knows: There is a paragraph missing. A short, critical paragraph — sometimes only two or three sentences — that sits between the final argument and the closing formula. This paragraph does not introduce new information. It does not add a new argument. It does something far more important.

It tells the reader: "Here is where we stand. Here is what I expect. Here is what happens next."

This is the pre-closing paragraph — and skipping it is like ending a business meeting by standing up and walking out without saying what the next steps are.

1️⃣ WHAT IS THE PRE-CLOSING PARAGRAPH?

Definition

The pre-closing paragraph is the final content paragraph of your letter, positioned immediately before the closing formula (formule de politesse). It is typically 20–40 words long — shorter than your body paragraphs but structurally indispensable.

The Three Functions It Performs

FunctionWhat It DoesWhy It Matters
1. SummaryRestates your position or purpose in one condensed sentenceEnsures the examiner's last content impression is your core message
2. ExpectationStates what you hope or expect will happen nextTransforms your letter from a monologue into a dialogue — you are inviting a response
3. AvailabilitySignals your willingness to continue the conversationShows professionalism and openness, preventing the letter from feeling like an ultimatum

Where It Sits in the Letter Architecture

Recall the visual template from Ch. 1:

[PARAGRAPH 3 — Action: your request, solution, expectation]

[PRE-CLOSING — summary, looking forward, availability]    ← THIS CHAPTER

[CLOSING FORMULA — formule de politesse]                   ← Ch. 33-34
[SIGNATURE]                                                ← Ch. 35

💡 Structural choice: The pre-closing can be either (a) the final sentences of Paragraph 3, flowing naturally from your request/solution, or (b) a separate short paragraph of its own. Both are acceptable. Option (b) — a separate short paragraph — is generally stronger because it visually signals to the examiner that you are concluding deliberately, not just running out of things to say.

2️⃣ WHY THIS ELEMENT MATTERS TO EXAMINERS

The Recency Effect — Revisited

In Ch. 4, we discussed the recency effect: the psychological principle that the last thing a reader encounters disproportionately shapes their overall impression. The closing formula (Ch. 33–34) is the very last sentence, but it is a formula — it demonstrates sociolinguistic competence, not content mastery.

The pre-closing paragraph is the last piece of original content the examiner reads. It is the last moment where your thinking, your argumentation, and your strategic communication are on display.

Consider two scenarios:

Scenario A — No pre-closing paragraph:

...Je vous demande donc de procéder au remboursement intégral de ma commande.

Veuillez agréer, Madame, Monsieur, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées.

The letter ends abruptly. The demand hangs in the air with no softening, no forward-looking statement, no availability signal. The examiner's impression: functional but blunt.

Scenario B — With pre-closing paragraph:

...Je vous demande donc de procéder au remboursement intégral de ma commande.

Je reste à votre entière disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire et espère que nous pourrons trouver une issue favorable à cette situation dans les meilleurs délais.

Veuillez agréer, Madame, Monsieur, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées.

The letter breathes. The demand is followed by openness. The examiner's impression: professional, complete, mature.

The Examiner's Internal Checklist at This Point

When the examiner reaches the end of your letter, they unconsciously ask:

QuestionWhat They're Checking
"Did the candidate wrap up, or just stop?"Coherence — does the letter have a deliberate conclusion?
"Do I know what the candidate expects to happen next?"Task completion — was the communicative purpose fulfilled?
"Does the tone remain consistent through the end?"Sociolinguistic appropriateness — no register collapse at the finish line?
"Is the transition to the closing formula smooth?"Cohesion — does the pre-closing lead naturally into "Veuillez agréer..."?

A letter without a pre-closing paragraph answers "no" to at least two of these questions.

3️⃣ THE ANATOMY OF A PRE-CLOSING PARAGRAPH

Every effective pre-closing paragraph contains one, two, or all three of the following elements, combined into one to three sentences:

Element A: The "Looking Forward" Statement

This expresses your expectation or hope regarding the outcome.

Pattern: "Dans l'attente de..." / "J'espère que..." / "Je compte sur..."

This element is almost always present. It is the backbone of the pre-closing.

Element B: The "Availability" Statement

This signals that you are open to further communication.

Pattern: "Je reste à votre disposition pour..." / "Je me tiens à votre disposition pour..." / "N'hésitez pas à me contacter si..."

This element appears in most letter types, especially complaints, requests, and cover letters.

Element C: The "Attached Documents" Reference

This mentions any documents you are including with the letter.

Pattern: "Vous trouverez ci-joint..." / "Je vous prie de trouver ci-joint..."

This element appears only when relevant — when the prompt mentions documents, receipts, a CV, photos, etc.

Combination Patterns

PatternElements UsedBest ForExample Length
A aloneLooking forward onlyOpinion letters, short letters approaching word limit15–20 words
A + BLooking forward + AvailabilityComplaints, requests, suggestions25–35 words
A + B + CLooking forward + Availability + DocumentsComplaints with receipts, cover letters with CV30–45 words
B + AAvailability first, then looking forwardCover letters (availability is the key message)25–35 words

⚠️ Register note on "Dans l'attente de...": This expression is a participial phrase — it has no conjugated verb. It is grammatically incomplete on its own. It must be followed by either (a) the closing formula in the same sentence, or (b) a main clause. Both are correct:

Option 1 (pre-closing flows into closing formula):
Dans l'attente de votre réponse, je vous prie d'agréer, Madame, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées.

Option 2 (pre-closing is separate, then closing formula follows):
Dans l'attente de votre réponse, je reste à votre disposition pour tout complément d'information.
Veuillez agréer, Madame, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées.

Both are B2-appropriate. Option 1 is more elegant and is perceived as slightly more formal. Option 2 is safer because it separates the elements clearly.

5️⃣ COMPLETE SENTENCE BANK — ELEMENT B: "AVAILABILITY" STATEMENTS

These sentences signal your openness to further contact. They are not letter-type specific — the same availability statements work across all letter types, with minor adjustments.

The Core Availability Formulas

FrenchLiteral MeaningRegisterNotes

💡 For TEF purposes, "Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire" is your safest, most reliable availability statement. It is universally appropriate, always correct, and signals exactly the right register. If you memorize only one pre-closing sentence, memorize this one.

The Grammar of "ci-joint"

⚠️ Agreement rule for ci-joint: This is a classic grammar trap that examiners notice.

Before the noun (no article), ci-joint is invariable:
Ci-joint les documents demandés. ✅
Ci-jointe les documents demandés. ❌

After the noun, ci-joint agrees:
Vous trouverez les documents ci-joints. ✅ (masculine plural)
Veuillez trouver la facture ci-jointe. ✅ (feminine singular)

After "Vous trouverez ci-joint" + noun with article — both invariable and agreeing forms are accepted in modern usage:
Vous trouverez ci-joint la copie de la facture. ✅ (invariable — traditional)
Vous trouverez ci-jointe la copie de la facture. ✅ (agreeing — modern)

For TEF, use the invariable form after "Vous trouverez ci-joint" — it is always correct and avoids any risk of wrong agreement.

7️⃣ COMBINING ELEMENTS — ASSEMBLY PATTERNS

Now that you have the three element banks, here is how to combine them into complete pre-closing paragraphs for each letter type.

Pattern 1: Complaint Letter Pre-Closing

Elements: A (looking forward — firm) + B (availability) + optional C (documents)

Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire et vous prie de trouver ci-joint une copie de la facture correspondante. Dans l'attente d'une réponse rapide de votre part, ...

Annotated breakdown:

SegmentElementFunction
Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaireB — AvailabilityShows openness, professionalism
et vous prie de trouver ci-joint une copie de la facture correspondanteC — DocumentsReferences enclosed proof
Dans l'attente d'une réponse rapide de votre part, ...A — Looking forwardSets expectation; leads into closing formula

Word count: ~35 words. Combined with the closing formula, the entire closing section is ~55–60 words.

Pattern 2: Request Letter Pre-Closing

Elements: B (availability) + A (looking forward — hopeful)

Je me tiens à votre disposition pour vous fournir tout document complémentaire qui pourrait s'avérer nécessaire. En espérant que ma demande pourra être prise en considération, ...

Pattern 3: Suggestion Letter Pre-Closing

Elements: A (looking forward — collaborative) + B (availability)

J'espère que ces propositions retiendront votre attention. Je reste bien entendu à votre disposition pour en discuter plus en détail lors d'une éventuelle réunion.

Pattern 4: Cover Letter Pre-Closing

Elements: B (availability — interview) + A (looking forward) + C (documents)

Vous trouverez ci-joint mon curriculum vitae détaillant l'ensemble de mon parcours. Je me tiens à votre entière disposition pour un entretien à votre convenance et espère avoir l'opportunité de vous présenter ma candidature de vive voix.

Pattern 5: Response Letter Pre-Closing (Accepting)

Elements: A (looking forward — positive) + B (availability)

Je me réjouis à la perspective de cette collaboration et reste à votre disposition pour convenir des modalités pratiques.

Pattern 6: Response Letter Pre-Closing (Declining)

Elements: A (looking forward — conciliatory) + B (availability — conditional)

J'espère que vous comprendrez les raisons de ma décision et que cette situation ne compromettra pas nos relations futures. Je reste néanmoins ouvert(e) à toute éventuelle collaboration ultérieure.

Pattern 7: Opinion Letter Pre-Closing

Elements: A (looking forward — reflective) only

J'espère que ces réflexions contribueront au débat et encourageront d'autres lecteurs à s'exprimer sur cette question essentielle.

💡 Notice: Opinion letters typically do NOT include an availability statement (Element B), because you are not inviting a personal response — you are contributing to public discourse. The pre-closing for opinion letters is therefore shorter and consists only of a forward-looking reflection.

8️⃣ THE GRAMMAR OF THE PRE-CLOSING

Tenses That Dominate This Section

The pre-closing paragraph uses a specific and predictable set of tenses. Understanding why each tense appears here prevents errors.

TenseWhy It Appears HereExample
Present indicativeStates your current availability and positionJe reste à votre disposition. / Je me tiens à votre disposition.
Future simpleExpresses formal expectation of what will happenVous trouverez ci-joint... / Je me rendrai disponible...
Conditional presentSoftens expressions of hope or desire (politeness)Je serais ravi(e) de pouvoir en discuter. / Je vous serais reconnaissant(e) de...
Subjunctive presentAfter expressions of wish or hope requiring subjunctiveJe souhaite que cette situation puisse se résoudre rapidement.
Present participle / GerundIn "En espérant que..." / "En vous remerciant de..." constructionsEn espérant que ma candidature retiendra votre attention...

The "En + present participle" Construction

This construction is extremely common in pre-closings and deserves special attention.

Formation: En + verb stem + -ant

Common pre-closing uses:

FrenchUnderlying VerbMeaning
En espérant que...espérerHoping that...
En vous remerciant de l'attention que vous porterez à...remercierThanking you for the attention you will give to...
En comptant sur votre compréhension...compterCounting on your understanding...
En vous remerciant par avance de votre réponse...remercierThanking you in advance for your response...

⚠️ Critical grammar rule: The "En + present participle" construction must share the same subject as the main clause that follows. In letter writing, this means the subject of the en + participle and the subject of the closing formula must be the same — which is always "je/vous" in the context of the closing formula. This rule is naturally satisfied in letter closings, but be aware of it:

✅ En espérant que ma demande retiendra votre attention, je vous prie d'agréer... (Subject of both: je)

❌ En espérant que ma demande retiendra votre attention, veuillez agréer... — This is technically a dangling participle because "en espérant" implies "je" but "veuillez" addresses "vous." However, this construction is so entrenched in French letter-writing convention that it is universally accepted in formal correspondence. Do not worry about this for TEF purposes. Both versions are correct in epistolary usage.

The "Dans l'attente de" Construction

This is a nominal phrase (built around a noun, not a verb). It functions as a circumstantial complement.

Pattern: Dans l'attente de + noun or Dans l'attente de + infinitive

ConstructionExampleNotes
Dans l'attente de + nounDans l'attente de votre réponse, ...Most common form
Dans l'attente de + infinitiveDans l'attente de vous lire, ...Slightly less common but elegant
Dans l'attente de + noun + relative clauseDans l'attente d'une réponse que j'espère favorable, ...More complex — B2/C1 level

What follows "Dans l'attente de..."?

It must be followed by a main clause. That main clause can be:

  1. The closing formula itself: Dans l'attente de votre réponse, je vous prie d'agréer...
  2. An availability statement: Dans l'attente de votre réponse, je reste à votre disposition...
  3. A separate sentence: Dans l'attente de votre réponse. ❌ INCORRECT — this is a sentence fragment. "Dans l'attente de" cannot stand alone.

9️⃣ THE TRANSITION FROM PARAGRAPH 3 TO THE PRE-CLOSING

The Problem

You have just written Paragraph 3, which contained your demand, request, solution, or call to action (Ch. 26–30). Now you need to transition to the pre-closing without:

  • Repeating yourself
  • Introducing new arguments
  • Breaking the tonal flow
  • Sounding abrupt

The Solution: Three Transition Techniques

Technique 1: The Connector Bridge

Use a concluding connector to signal that you are wrapping up.

ConnectorMeaningExample
En résuméIn summaryEn résumé, je vous demande de procéder au remplacement de l'appareil défectueux. Je reste à votre disposition...
En définitiveUltimatelyEn définitive, cette situation nécessite une intervention rapide de votre part.
C'est pourquoiThat is whyC'est pourquoi je fais appel à votre bienveillance pour examiner ma demande. J'espère que...

Technique 2: The Paragraph Break

Simply start a new paragraph. The visual break itself signals the transition. No connector needed.

[End of Paragraph 3 — last sentence of your argument/request]

[New paragraph — pre-closing begins]

This is the simplest and most reliable technique. The paragraph break does the work. The examiner sees the shift and reads the pre-closing as a conclusion.

Technique 3: The Tonal Shift

Your Paragraph 3 was action-oriented (demands, conditions, requests). Your pre-closing shifts to a softer, more diplomatic tone. The shift in tone itself creates the transition.

Paragraph 3 ending (firm): ...Je vous demande donc de procéder au remboursement intégral de ma commande dans un délai de quinze jours.

Pre-closing (softened): Je reste néanmoins à votre disposition pour trouver toute solution qui vous semblerait plus adaptée.

The word "néanmoins" and the conditional "semblerait" signal the tonal shift from demand to openness.

🔟 WHAT COULD GO WRONG — PRE-CLOSING ERRORS

Error 1: Introducing New Information

❌ Je reste à votre disposition. Par ailleurs, je tiens à mentionner que le livreur a été très impoli lors de la livraison. Dans l'attente de votre réponse...

Why it's wrong: The impoliteness of the delivery person is new information that should have appeared in Paragraph 2 (argument development). Introducing it in the pre-closing feels chaotic and suggests poor planning. The examiner reads this as a coherence failure.

Fix: Move the new point to Paragraph 2 or 3. The pre-closing should contain zero new facts.

Error 2: Skipping the Pre-Closing Entirely

❌ ...Je vous demande un remboursement complet.
Veuillez agréer, Madame, Monsieur, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées.

Why it's wrong: The jump from demand to closing formula is abrupt. There is no bridge. The letter feels unfinished — like a conversation that ends mid-thought.

Fix: Add even one sentence of pre-closing: Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire. This single sentence transforms the ending.

Error 3: The Pre-Closing That's Too Long

❌ Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire. J'espère que vous comprendrez ma frustration face à cette situation. Je suis un client fidèle depuis de nombreuses années et je n'avais jamais rencontré un tel problème. J'espère vraiment que vous pourrez résoudre cette situation rapidement. Je vous remercie par avance de l'attention que vous porterez à ma demande. Vous trouverez ci-joint une copie de ma facture. Dans l'attente de votre réponse rapide...

Why it's wrong: This is not a pre-closing — it's an entire additional paragraph. It repeats sentiments already expressed (frustration, loyalty, hope for resolution). The pre-closing should be 20–40 words, not 80+.

Fix: Select ONE looking-forward statement, ONE availability statement, and ONE document reference (if needed). Cut everything else.

Error 4: Register Collapse

❌ Bref, j'espère que ça va s'arranger vite. Contactez-moi quand vous voulez.

Why it's wrong: "Bref" is informal. "Ça" is informal (should be "cela" or restructured). "Contactez-moi quand vous voulez" is too direct and casual. After three paragraphs of formal French, this register collapse is devastating — it undoes the examiner's positive impression.

Fix: En espérant que cette situation pourra se résoudre dans les meilleurs délais, je reste à votre disposition pour tout échange complémentaire.

Error 5: Contradicting Your Own Tone

❌ [After a very firm Paragraph 3 demanding immediate refund]:
Je comprendrais tout à fait si vous ne pouviez pas accéder à ma demande.

Why it's wrong: You just spent an entire paragraph making a firm, justified demand. Now you're saying you'd understand if they refuse. This undermines your entire argument. The pre-closing should maintain the position established in Paragraph 3, not retreat from it.

Fix: Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire et espère que nous pourrons trouver une issue favorable dans les meilleurs délais. — This is open without being weak.

1️⃣2️⃣ B1 vs. B2 vs. C1 — THE SAME PRE-CLOSING AT THREE LEVELS

Scenario: Complaint about a defective product, requesting replacement

B1 Level:

Merci de me répondre vite. Je suis disponible par téléphone.

What marks this as B1:

  • "Merci de me répondre vite" — informal, uses "merci de" + infinitive (giving an instruction disguised as thanks)
  • "vite" — informal; the formal equivalent is "dans les meilleurs délais" or "rapidement"
  • "Je suis disponible par téléphone" — functional but flat; no formal phrasing
  • No "Dans l'attente de" or "Je reste à votre disposition" — the signature formal expressions are absent
  • Two short, choppy sentences — no syntactic complexity

B2 Level:

Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire. Dans l'attente d'une réponse favorable de votre part, j'espère que nous pourrons trouver une issue satisfaisante à cette situation dans les meilleurs délais.

What marks this as B2:

  • "Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire" — the key formal availability formula
  • "Dans l'attente de" — formal participial construction
  • "une réponse favorable" — formal adjective placement
  • "j'espère que nous pourrons" — conditional softening through "pouvoir" in future
  • "une issue satisfaisante" — formal vocabulary (issue = outcome, not "issue" in English)
  • "dans les meilleurs délais" — formal time expression replacing "vite"
  • Two sentences with varied length and structure

C1 Level:

Je demeure à votre entière disposition pour vous fournir tout élément complémentaire susceptible de faciliter le traitement de ma réclamation. Dans l'attente d'une réponse que j'ose espérer diligente, je forme le vœu que cette regrettable mésaventure trouvera une issue à la hauteur de la réputation de votre enseigne.

What marks this as C1:

  • "Je demeure" — literary register verb replacing "je reste"
  • "à votre entière disposition" — intensified with "entière"
  • "susceptible de faciliter" — formal adjectival construction
  • "le traitement de ma réclamation" — nominalization (turning a verb concept into a noun phrase)
  • "que j'ose espérer diligente" — embedded relative clause with "oser"
  • "je forme le vœu que" — elevated expression replacing "j'espère que"
  • "cette regrettable mésaventure" — sophisticated vocabulary (mésaventure = misadventure)
  • "à la hauteur de la réputation de votre enseigne" — elegant comparative that flatters while demanding
  • Complex syntax with multiple embedded clauses

💡 Your target is the B2 version. It is complete, correct, appropriately formal, and scores well on every criterion. The C1 version is aspirational — notice how it uses the same structure (availability + looking forward) but with richer vocabulary and more complex syntax.

1️⃣3️⃣ THE PRE-CLOSING AS WORD COUNT MANAGEMENT TOOL

A Strategic Function

Beyond its communicative purpose, the pre-closing paragraph serves a practical exam function: it is your word count adjustment tool.

After writing Paragraphs 1–3, you should have approximately 160–200 words. If you are:

SituationWord Count After P3Pre-Closing Strategy
Under target (160–180 words)Need 20–40 more content wordsUse a full three-element pre-closing (A + B + C) — this adds 35–45 words
On target (190–210 words)Need only a brief conclusionUse a two-element pre-closing (A + B) — this adds 20–30 words
Over target (220+ words)Need to wrap up quicklyUse a single-element pre-closing (A alone, folded into the closing formula) — this adds only 10–15 words

The Minimal Pre-Closing

If you are running long, the absolute minimum is:

Dans l'attente de votre réponse, je vous prie d'agréer...

Here, the "looking forward" element (A) is fused directly with the closing formula. It is only ~8 words of pre-closing content, but it is enough to bridge the gap.

The Expanded Pre-Closing

If you need more words, the maximum before it becomes excessive:

Je reste à votre entière disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire et vous prie de trouver ci-joint une copie de la facture ainsi que les photographies attestant de l'état du produit à la réception. En espérant que cette situation pourra être résolue à notre satisfaction mutuelle dans les plus brefs délais, ...

This is ~45 words of pre-closing content. Beyond this, you are over-extending.

1️⃣4️⃣ THE LINK BETWEEN PRE-CLOSING AND CLOSING FORMULA

This is the critical structural connection that Chapter 33 will develop fully, but you must understand the principle now.

The Fusion Option

The pre-closing and closing formula can be fused into a single sentence:

Dans l'attente de votre réponse, je vous prie d'agréer, Madame, Monsieur, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées.

In this construction:

  • "Dans l'attente de votre réponse" = pre-closing (Element A)
  • "je vous prie d'agréer..." = closing formula

They form one grammatical sentence. The comma after "réponse" connects them.

The Separation Option

The pre-closing and closing formula can be two separate sentences / paragraphs:

Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire et espère que nous pourrons trouver une issue favorable à cette situation.

Veuillez agréer, Madame, Monsieur, l'expression de mes salutations distinguées.

In this construction:

  • The pre-closing is a complete, self-contained paragraph
  • The closing formula stands alone as the final sentence

Which Option to Choose?

OptionAdvantageDisadvantageBest For
FusionElegant, concise, demonstrates syntactic skillCan become a very long sentence; harder to controlCandidates confident in complex syntax
SeparationClear, safe, easy to controlSlightly less fluidCandidates who want maximum clarity and safety

💡 For TEF, the separation option is recommended. It is easier to execute correctly under exam pressure, and it ensures that neither the pre-closing nor the closing formula is compromised by the other. Chapter 33 will teach you the closing formula in detail — keeping it separate from the pre-closing means you can focus on each element independently.

Every other chapter teaches you how to argue, request, complain, suggest, or close. This chapter teaches you how to stop arguing and start concluding — a skill that is fundamentally different from both argumentation and formulaic closing.

The pre-closing paragraph is:

  1. Short — 20–40 words

  5. The last original content the examiner reads — the closing formula is a formula; the pre-closing is you

Universal availability: Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire.

Universal looking forward: Dans l'attente de votre réponse, ...

Universal combined: Je reste à votre disposition pour tout renseignement complémentaire. Dans l'attente d'une réponse favorable de votre part, ...
