📖 THE EXAM MOMENT

Your header is complete: sender, recipient, date, Objet. Your salutation sits on its own line, followed by a blank line. Your pen hovers over the blank space where your first paragraph will begin.

This is the moment of maximum psychological impact.

Everything before this — the header, the Objet, the salutation — was formatting. The examiner registered it visually. Now, for the first time, they are about to read your French. Not scan it. Not register it as a visual element. Read it — word by word, structure by structure, registering vocabulary choices, grammar decisions, and register signals.

Your first sentence is your verbal entrance. Think of it as walking into a room for a job interview. You have dressed appropriately (the header). You have shaken hands properly (the salutation). Now you open your mouth and speak. The first words that come out determine whether the interviewer leans forward with interest or leans back with concern.

The examiner, having already formed a preliminary impression from the visual scan (Ch. 4), now either confirms that impression ("Yes, this candidate is competent — the first sentence proves it") or revises it ("The formatting was good, but the language is weaker than expected").

A strong first sentence locks in the positive anchor. A weak first sentence weakens it. And because of confirmation bias (Ch. 4), the impression formed in this moment colors the reading of everything that follows.

This chapter gives you the exact first sentences for every letter type — not just the words, but the reasoning behind each formulation: why this structure, why this vocabulary, why this tense, and what each element signals to the examiner.

1️⃣ WHAT THE FIRST SENTENCE MUST ACCOMPLISH

The first sentence after the salutation must perform three simultaneous functions in one sentence:

FunctionWhat It MeansWhy It Matters
1. Identify your purposeThe examiner must know WHY you are writing after reading this sentenceRC — task identification begins here
2. Set your toneThe emotional/professional register of the letter is establishedSOC — the examiner reads tone from the first sentence
3. Demonstrate formal registerThe sentence must contain at least one clear formal register markerSOC, VOC — the examiner's first language-level assessment happens here

The Three Functions in Action

Consider this first sentence:

Je me permets de vous écrire au sujet de la commande n° 4521, dont la livraison a fait l'objet d'un retard considérable.

FunctionHow This Sentence Fulfills It
PurposeThe reader knows this is about order #4521 and a delivery delay — the purpose is immediately clear
ToneThe phrase "retard considérable" signals displeasure without aggression — measured, formal complaint tone
Register"Je me permets de" (formal opening formula), "au sujet de" (formal preposition), "a fait l'objet de" (formal verbal construction), "considérable" (formal adjective) — four register markers in one sentence

Now consider this weak first sentence:

Je vous écris parce que j'ai un problème avec ma commande.

FunctionHow This Sentence Fails
PurposeVaguely identified — "a problem with my order" gives no specifics
ToneNeutral to informal — no formal tone established
Register"parce que" (basic connector), "j'ai un problème" (informal phrasing), "ma commande" (no reference number, no formal vocabulary) — zero register markers

🔴 The difference between these two sentences is the difference between a B2 first impression and a B1 first impression. The content is roughly the same (complaint about an order). The execution is radically different. This chapter teaches you the execution.

2️⃣ THE UNIVERSAL OPENING STRUCTURE — "JE ME PERMETS DE"

The Most Powerful First-Sentence Opener in French Formal Writing

Before we examine letter-type-specific openings, you need to understand the single most versatile and reliable opening formula in French formal correspondence:

Je me permets de vous écrire...

This phrase literally means "I permit myself to write to you" — which in English sounds odd, but in French communicates something very specific:

What It Literally SaysWhat It Actually Signals
"I am taking the liberty of writing to you"Respectful initiative — I am reaching out, but I acknowledge that I am imposing on your time. I am polite enough to frame my initiative as a permission I grant myself, not a right I claim.

Why This Formula Is So Effective

DimensionHow "Je me permets de" Scores
SOCImmediately signals formal register — this phrase exists ONLY in formal French. No one says "je me permets de" in casual conversation.
VOCDemonstrates a formal reflexive construction that goes beyond basic vocabulary
GRAMCorrect use of the reflexive pronoun me + the verb permettre + the infinitive construction — shows grammatical competence
ToneSets a respectful, measured tone — appropriate for any letter type
PrimacyThe examiner reads this and immediately thinks: "This candidate knows formal French." Positive anchor locked in.

Variations of "Je me permets de"

VariationRegister LevelWhen to Use
Je me permets de vous écrire...🔵 FormalUniversal — works for any letter type
Je me permets de vous adresser le présent courrier...🔵🔵 Very formalWhen writing to authorities or in very formal contexts
Je me permets de vous contacter...🔵 FormalSlightly less formal than écrire — acceptable
Je me permets de porter à votre attention...🔵🔵 Very formalWhen reporting or signaling a problem to an authority
Je me permets de solliciter votre attention...🔵🔵 Very formalWhen making an important request
Je me permets de vous faire part de...🔵 FormalWhen sharing information, concerns, or suggestions

What Follows "Je me permets de vous écrire"

The formula needs a purpose complement — a phrase that specifies WHY you are writing:

Purpose ComplementStructureExample
About a topic...au sujet de + [noun phrase]Je me permets de vous écrire au sujet de la commande n° 4521.
Concerning a topic...concernant + [noun phrase]Je me permets de vous écrire concernant la panne d'ascenseur de notre immeuble.
In order to...afin de + [infinitive]Je me permets de vous écrire afin de solliciter une modification de mes horaires de travail.
To express...pour vous faire part de + [noun phrase]Je me permets de vous écrire pour vous faire part de mon mécontentement.
Following...à la suite de + [noun phrase]Je me permets de vous écrire à la suite de l'incident survenu le 15 mars dernier.

💡 Memorize "Je me permets de vous écrire" as your default opening. It works for complaints, requests, suggestions, and opinion letters. For cover letters and response letters, different openings are more appropriate (see Sections 7 and 8 below). But when in doubt, "Je me permets de vous écrire" is ALWAYS safe.

3️⃣ ALTERNATIVE UNIVERSAL OPENERS

While "Je me permets de" is the most reliable, variety is valuable. Here are other formal opening structures that work across multiple letter types:

The "Following" Opener — "À la suite de / Suite à"

StructureExampleBest For
À la suite de + [event/document]À la suite de l'incident survenu le 15 mars dans votre établissement, je souhaiterais porter à votre connaissance les faits suivants.Complaints triggered by a specific event
Suite à + [event/document]Suite à notre conversation téléphonique du 10 mars dernier, je me permets de vous adresser le présent courrier.Follow-ups to previous contact
Faisant suite à + [document]Faisant suite à votre courrier du 5 mars, je souhaiterais vous apporter les précisions suivantes.Responses to received letters

The "As a" Opener — "En tant que"

StructureExampleBest For
En tant que + [role], je me permets de...En tant que résidente du quartier Saint-Michel depuis dix ans, je me permets de vous écrire au sujet des nuisances sonores qui affectent notre quotidien.Any letter where your role/standing is relevant (suggestions to mayors, parent letters to schools, resident letters to landlords)

This opener is powerful because it immediately establishes your standing — your right to write this letter. The examiner sees: "This person has identified their role and established their legitimacy."

The "Having" Opener — Participial Construction

StructureExampleBest For
Ayant + [past participle], je...Ayant constaté d'importantes dégradations dans les parties communes de notre immeuble, je me permets de vous écrire afin de vous alerter sur cette situation.Complaints and signalements where you have personally observed something
Ayant pris connaissance de + [information]Ayant pris connaissance de l'offre d'emploi parue sur votre site le 12 avril dernier, je souhaiterais vous présenter ma candidature.Cover letters (very common and effective)
Ayant été informé(e) de + [decision/event]Ayant été informée de votre décision de supprimer les cours de musique, je me permets de vous faire part de ma vive préoccupation.Reaction letters (to announced decisions)

💡 The participial opening (Ayant + past participle) is a C1-level structure that is surprisingly easy to execute. It signals grammatical sophistication immediately. If you are comfortable with past participle agreement, use it. If not, stick with "Je me permets de" — which is perfectly adequate at B2.

The "I wish to" Opener — "Je souhaiterais"

StructureExampleBest For
Je souhaiterais + [infinitive]Je souhaiterais attirer votre attention sur une situation particulièrement préoccupante concernant l'état de l'ascenseur de notre immeuble.Any letter — very versatile conditional opening
Je souhaiterais vous faire part de...Je souhaiterais vous faire part de mes observations concernant le projet de réaménagement du centre-ville.Suggestions and opinion letters

The "I have the honor" Opener — "J'ai l'honneur de"

StructureExampleBest For
J'ai l'honneur de + [infinitive]J'ai l'honneur de porter à votre connaissance les difficultés rencontrées par les résidents de notre quartier.Very formal letters to high-ranking officials (mayor, prefect)
J'ai l'honneur de solliciter...J'ai l'honneur de solliciter votre bienveillante attention sur le dossier suivant.Formal requests to authorities

⚠️ "J'ai l'honneur de" is very formal — almost ceremonial. Use it ONLY when writing to elected officials or very high-ranking authorities. Using it in a consumer complaint to a company would sound disproportionate and slightly ridiculous. Reserve it for letters to mayors, prefects, or institutional presidents.

HOOK 1: THE COMPLAINT FIRST SENTENCE

The Psychological Goal

The first sentence of a complaint letter must accomplish a delicate balance: identify the problem clearly while maintaining composure. You are upset, but you are in control. You are firm, but you are polite. The examiner must read this sentence and think: "This person has a legitimate grievance and is expressing it professionally."

What to Avoid in a Complaint Opening

❌ Do NOT Start With...Why Not
"Je suis très mécontent(e)..."Starting with your EMOTION puts feeling before facts. Formal complaints start with FACTS, then express reaction.
"Je vous écris pour me plaindre...""Me plaindre" sounds whiny in French. Use "formuler une réclamation" or "porter à votre attention" instead.
"C'est inadmissible que..."Starting with a judgment before establishing facts sounds aggressive. Establish facts first, then judge.
"J'ai un problème avec..."Too basic, too informal. "Un problème" is vague. "J'ai" is a B1 construction.

Cover Letter First Sentence — What NOT to Do

❌ WrongWhy✅ Correct Replacement
Je vous écris pour le travail.Vague, informal, no specific positionJe souhaiterais vous présenter ma candidature au poste de chargé(e) de communication.

🔴 THE "EXCITÉ" TRAP: This is the single most dangerous anglicism in French cover letters. English speakers naturally want to say "I am excited to apply." In French, excité(e) has a sexual connotation. The correct alternatives are: enthousiaste ("enthusiastic"), vivement intéressé(e) ("keenly interested"), or motivé(e) ("motivated"). Never, under any circumstances, write "Je suis excité(e)" in a formal French letter.

PresentTo state your current action (writing) or the current situationJe me permets de vous écrire...The act of writing is happening now
Conditional presentTo express polite wishes or requestsJe souhaiterais vous présenter ma candidature...Conditional = politeness mode (Ch. 3)
Passé composéTo reference a past event that triggered the letterJ'ai pris connaissance de votre annonce...The event (seeing the ad) happened in the past

7️⃣ WHAT COULD GO WRONG — FIRST SENTENCE ERRORS

Error 1: Starting with "Je" as the Very First Word

What it looks like:

Je vous écris pour vous informer de...

Why it's problematic: Starting with Je is not grammatically wrong, but it is stylistically weak. In French formal writing, beginning directly with Je can feel abrupt and self-centered. The formal alternatives ("Je me permets de," "Ayant pris connaissance de," "En tant que," "C'est avec...") create a more measured, sophisticated opening.

Impact: VOC − (missed opportunity for formal vocabulary), SOC − (slightly informal feel)

Fix: Use one of the formal openers from this chapter. Even "Je me permets de vous écrire" starts with Je but softens it with the reflexive construction.

⚠️ This is a MODERATE concern, not a catastrophic one. Starting with Je is far better than starting with Bonjour or an informal expression. But if you can use a participial opening (Ayant...), a prepositional opening (En tant que...), or a literary opening (C'est avec...), you create a stronger impression.

Error 2: Copying the Prompt Verbatim

What it looks like:

Prompt: "Vous avez commandé un appareil électroménager sur le site Internet de la société Électro-Plus."

First sentence: "J'ai commandé un appareil électroménager sur le site Internet de la société Électro-Plus."

Why it's wrong: The candidate has simply changed "Vous avez" to "J'ai" and copied the rest word for word. This demonstrates zero paraphrasing ability — a B1 limitation. The examiner notices immediately that the sentence is lifted from the prompt.

Impact: VOC − (no vocabulary demonstration — the words are the prompt's, not yours), RC − (copying suggests inability to process and reformulate)

Fix: Paraphrase using your own vocabulary and structure:

Prompt TextCandidate's Paraphrase
"Vous avez commandé un appareil électroménager sur le site Internet de la société Électro-Plus""Je me permets de vous écrire au sujet de la commande n° 4521, passée le 15 mars dernier sur votre site, qui concernait un appareil électroménager."

The paraphrase reorganizes the information, adds formal vocabulary (je me permets de, au sujet de, passée le), and demonstrates that the candidate can process the prompt, not just copy it.

Error 3: Being Too Long (First Sentence Over 40 Words)

What it looks like:

Je me permets de vous écrire au sujet de la commande n° 4521, passée sur votre site Internet le 15 mars dernier, pour un appareil électroménager de marque CleanPro, modèle X500, qui est arrivé endommagé, avec la façade fissurée et le bouton de mise en marche cassé, après un retard de livraison de trois semaines par rapport à la date initialement annoncée, et dont je souhaiterais vous faire part de mon mécontentement.

Why it's wrong: This is one sentence of 70+ words. The examiner loses track of the main clause. Cognitive fluency drops (Ch. 4). The sentence tries to accomplish everything at once — context, description, and reaction — when it should only accomplish purpose identification.

Fix: The first sentence identifies the PURPOSE. Details go in the rest of Paragraph 1 and in Paragraph 2. Aim for 20-35 words in the first sentence.

Too Long (70+ words)Right Length (25-30 words)
(the monster sentence above)Je me permets de vous écrire au sujet de la commande n° 4521, dont la livraison a fait l'objet d'un retard considérable et dont le produit est arrivé endommagé.

Error 4: Starting with Emotion Instead of Facts

What it looks like:

Je suis absolument furieux et scandalisé par le traitement que j'ai reçu de la part de votre entreprise.

Why it's wrong: Starting with emotion (furieux, scandalisé) before establishing facts makes the letter feel like a rant rather than a formal complaint. In French formal convention, facts come first, then measured reaction.

Fix: Establish the situation first, then express your reaction in measured terms:

Je me permets de vous écrire au sujet de la commande n° 4521, dont le traitement m'a vivement contrarié(e).

Vivement contrarié(e) ("deeply displeased") expresses the same emotion as furieux et scandalisé but in formal, measured language.

Error 5: Using Informal Vocabulary in the First Sentence

What it looks like:

Je vous écris parce que j'ai un truc qui marche pas et du coup je suis pas content.

Impact: SOC − (catastrophic register break in the first sentence — the highest-visibility position). The examiner's anchor shifts to "weak" immediately. Recovery is extremely difficult.

Fix: Every word in the first sentence must be formal register. Use the sentence banks in this chapter. If you are unsure about a word, replace it with a simpler but neutral formal alternative.

8️⃣ B1 vs. B2 vs. C1 — THE FIRST SENTENCE AT THREE LEVELS

Same Scenario: Complaint About a Damaged Product

B1 First Sentence:

Je vous écris parce que j'ai reçu un produit cassé.

Analysis:

  • Parce que — basic connector
  • J'ai reçu — basic structure
  • Un produit cassé — basic vocabulary (cassé is informal; endommagé or défectueux is formal)
  • No formal opener
  • No specific reference (no order number, no date)
  • No register markers

B2 First Sentence:

Je me permets de vous écrire au sujet de la commande n° 4521, passée le 15 mars dernier sur votre site Internet, dont le produit livré présentait de nombreux dommages à la réception.

Analysis:

  • Je me permets de — formal opener ✅
  • Au sujet de — formal preposition ✅
  • Specific reference (n° 4521, date) ✅
  • Présentait de nombreux dommages — formal vocabulary (dommages instead of cassé) ✅
  • À la réception — formal time marker ✅
  • Measured tone — factual, not emotional ✅

C1 First Sentence:

C'est avec une profonde déception que je me vois contraint de vous adresser le présent courrier, à la suite de la livraison de la commande n° 4521, dont l'article reçu s'est avéré gravement endommagé et non conforme à la description figurant sur votre site.

Analysis:

  • C'est avec une profonde déception que — literary opening ✅
  • Je me vois contraint de — very formal ("I find myself compelled to") ✅
  • Le présent courrier — elevated vocabulary ✅
  • S'est avéré — formal verb ("turned out to be") ✅
  • Non conforme à la description — legal-formal vocabulary ✅
  • Figurant sur votre site — present participle used as adjective ✅
  • Complex sentence structure with multiple embedded clauses ✅
