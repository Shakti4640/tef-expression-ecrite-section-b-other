📖 THE EXAM MOMENT

Your header is nearly complete. The sender block sits at the top-left (Ch. 6). The recipient block is positioned to the right (Ch. 7). The date line is in place (Ch. 8). You are about to cross the threshold from header to body.

Between these two zones sits a single line that most candidates either omit entirely or write so vaguely that it serves no purpose. This line is the Objet line — the subject line of your letter.

In email, you write a subject line instinctively. You know that "Meeting Tuesday" is a bad subject line and "Q2 Budget Review Meeting — Tuesday March 15 at 2pm" is a good one. You know that the subject line determines whether the email gets opened, read, or ignored.

The Objet line in a French formal letter serves the same function — but with higher stakes. In a TEF letter, the Objet line is the first piece of content the examiner reads. Before your salutation. Before your first sentence. Before your argument. The Objet line tells the examiner: "This is what this letter is about." If it is precise and well-written, the examiner knows immediately that you have understood the task. If it is vague, generic, or missing, the examiner enters your letter without a roadmap — and the primacy effect (Ch. 4) works against you.

This chapter teaches you what an Objet line is, why it matters disproportionately, how to format it, and how to write one for every possible TEF scenario — with a complete bank of 30+ ready-to-use examples.

1️⃣ WHAT THE OBJET LINE IS

Definition

The Objet line is a concise statement of the letter's purpose, placed between the date line and the salutation. It begins with the word Objet followed by a colon, then a noun phrase (not a complete sentence) that summarizes why you are writing.

The Anatomy

Objet : [Concise noun phrase describing the letter's purpose]
ComponentRule
The word "Objet"Always written with a capital O
The colonFollowed by a space (French spacing: space before AND after the colon)
The contentA noun phrase — NOT a full sentence. No verb conjugated to a subject. No period at the end.

What "Noun Phrase" Means

A noun phrase is a group of words centered on a noun. It does not contain a conjugated verb with a subject. It is a label, not a statement.

✅ Noun Phrase (Correct)❌ Full Sentence (Incorrect)
Réclamation concernant la commande n° 4521Je vous écris pour me plaindre de la commande n° 4521
Demande de renseignements sur les tarifs d'inscriptionJe souhaite obtenir des renseignements sur les tarifs
Candidature au poste de chargé de communicationJe voudrais poser ma candidature au poste de chargé de communication
Proposition d'aménagement d'un espace vertJe propose d'aménager un espace vert dans le quartier

The Objet line is a title, not a summary of your argument. It labels the letter's purpose the way a chapter title labels a chapter's content.

2️⃣ WHY THE OBJET LINE MATTERS DISPROPORTIONATELY

Reason 1: It Is the First Content the Examiner Reads

During the visual scan (Ch. 4), the examiner sees your header elements (sender, recipient, date). These are formatting elements — they signal convention awareness but do not reveal your understanding of the task. The Objet line is the first element that reveals your comprehension of the prompt. Before reading your salutation or first sentence, the examiner reads:

Objet : Réclamation concernant la livraison endommagée de la commande n° 4521

And immediately knows:

  • ✅ The candidate has identified this as a complaint (réclamation)
  • ✅ The candidate has identified the specific issue (livraison endommagée)
  • ✅ The candidate has included a reference number (n° 4521)
  • ✅ The candidate can write formal vocabulary (réclamation, concernant, endommagée)

All of this — task identification, specificity, vocabulary — from ONE line. The anchor (Ch. 4) is set to "competent" before the examiner reads a single paragraph.

Reason 2: It Demonstrates Vocabulary Precision

The Objet line forces you to use precise formal nouns — the exact kind of vocabulary that scores under VOC. Consider the difference:

Vague ObjetPrecise ObjetVocabulary Demonstrated
Objet : ProblèmeObjet : Réclamation relative à un dysfonctionnement du service Internetréclamation, relative à, dysfonctionnement
Objet : LettreObjet : Demande d'aménagement des horaires de travaildemande, aménagement, horaires
Objet : TravailObjet : Candidature au poste de chargé(e) de communication — réf. 2025-089candidature, poste, chargé(e) de communication, réf.

The precise Objet line contains 3-5 formal vocabulary items compressed into one line. This is the highest vocabulary-per-word-count ratio in the entire letter.

Reason 3: It Guides the Examiner's Reading

The Objet line creates a reading lens. When the examiner knows from the Objet that this is a "Réclamation concernant la commande n° 4521," they read the body expecting:

  • A description of what went wrong with order 4521
  • An expression of dissatisfaction
  • A request for resolution

This expectation alignment makes your letter easier to process (cognitive fluency — Ch. 4). The examiner's reading is smooth because they know what is coming. Your CC score benefits because the letter feels coherent — the Objet promised a complaint, and the body delivers one.

Reason 4: Its Absence Is Conspicuous

A letter without an Objet line is like a book without a title. The examiner notices the absence immediately during the visual scan. It creates a gap between the date line and the salutation that looks incomplete.

4️⃣ THE STRUCTURE OF AN EFFECTIVE OBJET LINE

The Formula

Every effective Objet line follows this structure:

Objet : [Type of letter] + [Specific subject] + [Reference/Detail if applicable]
ComponentWhat It DoesExample
Type of letterNames the nature of the correspondenceRéclamation, Demande, Candidature, Proposition, Réponse
Specific subjectIdentifies the particular issue or topicconcernant la commande n° 4521, relative aux horaires de travail, au poste de chargé de communication
Reference/DetailAdds precision when available— réf. 2025-089, — produit endommagé, — séjour du 15 au 18 mars

The Three Levels of Objet Line Quality

LevelExampleWhat It Signals
🔴 Vague (loses points)Objet : Lettre"This candidate cannot identify their own purpose" — RC −, VOC −
🟡 Adequate (acceptable)Objet : Réclamation"The candidate knows it's a complaint, but no specifics" — RC neutral, VOC minimal
🟢 Precise (scores points)Objet : Réclamation relative à la commande n° 4521 — retard de livraison et produit endommagé"The candidate has identified the type, the subject, and the specifics" — RC +, VOC +, SOC +

💡 Always aim for the precise level. The difference between adequate and precise is 3-5 additional words. Those 3-5 words demonstrate vocabulary, task comprehension, and formal convention awareness simultaneously.

8️⃣ COMMON OBJET LINE MISTAKES — AND HOW TO FIX THEM

Mistake 1: Omitting the Objet Line Entirely

How common: Very common (35-45% of candidates)

Why it happens: Candidates were never taught that French formal letters include an Objet line. Or they consider it optional. Or they forget it in the rush to start writing the body.

Impact: SOC − (missing convention), RC − (incomplete format). The examiner's visual scan (Ch. 4) detects the gap between the date and the salutation. Negative contribution to first impression.

Fix: The Objet line is part of your Phase 2 writing sequence. After writing the date, write the Objet IMMEDIATELY. It takes 15-30 seconds. It is never optional in a formal letter.

Mistake 2: Writing a Full Sentence Instead of a Noun Phrase

What it looks like:

Objet : Je vous écris pour me plaindre du retard de ma commande

Why it's wrong: The Objet line is a title, not a sentence. Titles are noun phrases. Writing a full sentence with a subject and conjugated verb turns the Objet into a preview of your first paragraph — which is redundant and formally incorrect.

Fix: Remove the subject and verb. Keep only the noun phrase:

Objet : Réclamation concernant le retard de livraison de la commande n° 4521

Mistake 3: Being Too Vague

What it looks like:

Objet : Problème
Objet : Lettre
Objet : Demande

Why it's wrong: These Objet lines tell the examiner nothing specific. "Problem" — what problem? "Letter" — every submission is a letter. "Request" — for what? The Objet line must identify the specific subject, not just the general category.

Fix: Add the specific subject:

Too VaguePrecise
Objet : ProblèmeObjet : Réclamation concernant la panne d'ascenseur non réparée
Objet : LettreObjet : Proposition d'amélioration du service de cantine scolaire
Objet : DemandeObjet : Demande de renseignements sur les conditions d'inscription au programme de formation

Mistake 4: Being Too Long

What it looks like:

Objet : Réclamation concernant la commande numéro 4521 passée le 15 mars 2025 sur votre site Internet pour un aspirateur de marque CleanPro modèle X500 qui est arrivé endommagé avec la façade fissurée et le bouton de mise en marche cassé après un retard de livraison de trois semaines

Why it's wrong: This is not an Objet line — it is a paragraph. The Objet should be concise: 5-15 words. It labels the subject; it does not narrate the entire story.

Fix: Compress to the essential information:

Objet : Réclamation concernant la commande n° 4521 — produit endommagé et retard de livraison

Mistake 5: Using the Wrong Noun for the Letter Type

What it looks like:

Objet : Réclamation (when the letter is actually a suggestion)
Objet : Demande (when the letter is actually a complaint)

Why it's wrong: The Objet line noun must match the letter type. Using réclamation for a suggestion letter signals that you have misidentified the task — an RC failure.

Fix: In Step 2 of your planning method (Ch. 5), you classify the letter type. The Objet line noun MUST correspond to this classification:

Letter TypeCorrect Objet Noun(s)
ComplaintRéclamation, Contestation, Signalement, Plainte
RequestDemande, Sollicitation
SuggestionProposition, Suggestion, Projet
ResponseRéponse, Suite, Acceptation
Cover letterCandidature
OpinionRéaction, Contribution, Point de vue, Courrier des lecteurs

Mistake 6: Writing "Objet" Incorrectly

ErrorCorrectWhy
Object :Objet :Object is English. Objet is French.
Sujet :Objet :Sujet means "subject" but is not the convention for French letter subject lines. The standard term is Objet.
RE :Objet :RE: is an email/English convention. Not used in French formal letters.
objet :Objet :Capital O is the convention for the start of this line.
OBJET :Objet : or OBJET :All-caps is acceptable in some administrative formats but Objet with initial capital is the most common convention.

Mistake 7: Adding a Period at the End

What it looks like:

Objet : Réclamation concernant la commande n° 4521.

Why it's wrong: The Objet line is a title/label, not a sentence. Titles do not end with periods. This is a minor error, but it signals that the candidate treats the Objet as a sentence rather than a heading.

Fix: No terminal punctuation. The line simply ends after the last word.

Mistake 8: Forgetting the Space Before the Colon

What it looks like:

Objet: Réclamation...

Why it's wrong: In French typography, a space is required BEFORE the colon (as well as after it). This is a French-specific rule that does not exist in English. Writing "Objet:" without a space before the colon applies English punctuation rules to French.

Correct: Objet : Réclamation... (space before AND after the colon)

⚠️ This French spacing rule applies to ALL colons in your letter, not just the Objet line. Whenever you write a colon, leave a space before it and after it. The same rule applies to semicolons (;), question marks (?), and exclamation marks (!). This was introduced in Ch. 3, Dimension 5 (Typographic Register).

9️⃣ ADVANCED TECHNIQUE: THE DOUBLE OBJET LINE

In some formal letters, the Objet line contains two elements separated by a dash or a line break. This is appropriate when the letter addresses two related issues or when you want to add a reference number.

Format

Objet : Réclamation concernant la commande n° 4521
        — retard de livraison et produit endommagé

Or on one line with a dash:

Objet : Réclamation — commande n° 4521 — produit endommagé

When to Use the Double Format

SituationSingle ObjetDouble Objet
Simple complaint about one issue✅ Objet : Réclamation concernant le retard de livraisonNot needed
Complaint about multiple issuesAcceptable but may feel incomplete✅ Objet : Réclamation — retard de livraison et produit endommagé — commande n° 4521
Cover letter with reference number✅ Objet : Candidature au poste de chargé de communication✅ Objet : Candidature au poste de chargé de communication — réf. 2025-089
Response to a dated letter✅ Objet : Réponse à votre courrier du 10 mars✅ Objet : Réponse à votre courrier du 10 mars — réf. 2025-0342

💡 The double format is a C1-level refinement. At B2 level, a single clear Objet line is fully sufficient. Use the double format only if you are confident in the structure and if the prompt provides enough information to warrant it.

1️⃣3️⃣ B1 vs. B2 vs. C1 — THE OBJET LINE AT THREE LEVELS

B1 Objet Line

Objet : Problème

Problems: Vague, generic, tells the examiner nothing specific. No formal vocabulary demonstrated. The examiner still does not know what the letter is about after reading the Objet. RC −, VOC −.

B2 Objet Line

Objet : Réclamation concernant la commande n° 4521 — produit endommagé

Correct: Specific type noun (réclamation), formal linking word (concernant), reference number (n° 4521), and specific issue (produit endommagé). The examiner knows exactly what the letter is about. RC ✅, VOC ✅, SOC ✅.

C1 Objet Line

Objet : Réclamation relative à la commande n° 4521 — retard de livraison 
        et non-conformité du produit reçu

Enhanced: More formal linking word (relative à instead of concernant), precise description of both issues (retard de livraison AND non-conformité du produit reçu), and the formal term non-conformité (non-compliance/non-conformity) which is a legal-administrative term demonstrating sophisticated vocabulary.

💡 Your target is B2. A precise Objet line with the correct type noun, a linking word, and the specific subject is fully sufficient. The C1 refinements (more formal linking words, legal vocabulary, double-issue format) are bonuses that can elevate your score but are not required.

1️⃣4️⃣ THE 30-SECOND OBJET LINE ROUTINE

On exam day, writing the Objet line should take no more than 30 seconds:

STEP A [10 seconds]: Look at your planning grid — what did you note for OBJET?
  → Keywords from planning: "Réclamation — commande 4521 — produit endommagé"

STEP B [5 seconds]: Choose your linking word
  → Default: "concernant" (always correct, no agreement needed)
  → Upgrade: "relative à / relatif à" (more formal, but requires gender agreement)

STEP C [15 seconds]: Write the Objet line
  → "Objet : " + [type noun] + [linking word] + [specific subject] + [reference if available]
  → "Objet : Réclamation concernant la commande n° 4521 — produit endommagé"

Done. Move to salutation (Ch. 10).
