📖 THE EXAM MOMENT — A THIRD COMMUNICATIVE POSTURE

You have now studied two complete letters. The complaint (Ch. 37) positioned you as an aggrieved party demanding resolution. The request (Ch. 38) positioned you as a petitioner asking for a favor. Now you face a third posture — one that is neither assertive nor deferential.

You are a citizen proposing an idea.

The suggestion letter is arguably the most intellectually demanding of the six letter types because it requires you to:

  1. Identify a real problem (observation)
  2. Analyze why it matters (argumentation)
  3. Propose a concrete solution (creativity)
  4. Explain how it could work (implementation)
  5. Do all of this while respecting the authority of the recipient — you are suggesting, not instructing

1️⃣ THE PROMPT

Situation :
Vous habitez dans le centre-ville de Toulouse depuis plusieurs années. Vous avez constaté que votre quartier manque d'espaces verts accessibles aux familles. Le seul parc existant est mal entretenu, les jeux pour enfants sont vétustes et il n'y a aucun espace de détente pour les personnes âgées. Vous estimez que l'aménagement d'un jardin partagé pourrait répondre à plusieurs de ces problèmes tout en renforçant le lien social entre les habitants.

Consigne :
Vous écrivez une lettre au maire de Toulouse pour lui proposer la création d'un jardin partagé dans votre quartier. Dans votre lettre, vous devez :

  • décrire la situation actuelle et les problèmes constatés ;
  • présenter votre proposition de jardin partagé et ses avantages ;
  • suggérer des modalités concrètes de mise en œuvre et proposer votre participation.

Rédigez une lettre formelle d'au moins 200 mots.

Objet : Proposition de création d'un jardin partagé — Quartier centre-ville

Monsieur le Maire,

En tant qu'habitante du centre-ville de Toulouse depuis plus de dix ans, je me permets de vous écrire afin d'attirer votre attention sur un constat partagé par de nombreux résidents de notre quartier. En effet, nous déplorons le manque criant d'espaces verts accessibles aux familles. Le seul parc existant souffre d'un entretien insuffisant, les aires de jeux pour enfants sont vétustes et aucun aménagement n'est prévu pour les personnes âgées, qui se trouvent ainsi privées d'un lieu de détente adapté à leurs besoins.

Face à cette situation, je souhaiterais vous soumettre une proposition qui, à mon sens, pourrait répondre à plusieurs de ces problématiques : la création d'un jardin partagé. Un tel espace offrirait aux habitants un lieu de rencontre intergénérationnel, favoriserait le lien social entre voisins et contribuerait à l'embellissement du quartier. De plus, il permettrait de sensibiliser les enfants au respect de l'environnement et de proposer aux personnes âgées une activité en plein air adaptée à leurs capacités.

Concernant la mise en œuvre de ce projet, le terrain actuellement inutilisé situé rue Matabiau, à proximité du parc existant, me semblerait constituer un emplacement idéal. Il conviendrait, dans un premier temps, de consulter les associations de quartier afin de recueillir les avis des résidents. Je me tiens personnellement prête à participer à l'organisation de ce projet et à coordonner un groupe de bénévoles parmi les habitants intéressés.

Je serais ravie de pouvoir vous présenter cette proposition plus en détail lors d'un éventuel rendez-vous. Dans l'espoir que cette initiative retiendra votre attention, je reste à votre entière disposition pour tout échange complémentaire.

Je vous prie d'agréer, Monsieur le Maire, l'expression de mes sentiments respectueux.

Claire MOREAU

Word count (content, excluding header): approximately 260 words

SegmentAnnotationTEF Criterion
Face à cette situation,"Faced with this situation." Transitional phrase — bridges from the problem description (P1) to the proposal (P2). "Face à" is the formal preposition for "in response to" / "confronted with." It positions the proposal as a direct response to the problems described.Coherence — clear transition from problem to solution
je souhaiterais vous soumettre une proposition"I would like to submit a proposal to you." Conditional (souhaiterais) — the same politeness structure from the request letter (Ch. 38), but here it serves a different purpose. In the request, the conditional expressed deference ("please help me"). In the suggestion, it expresses modesty ("I have an idea, if you are willing to consider it"). "Soumettre" (to submit) — the same verb used in Ch. 38 Sentence 6, here applied to a proposal rather than a candidature.Grammar — conditional for modesty; Vocabulary — soumettre une proposition (formal)
qui, à mon sens, pourrait répondre à plusieurs de ces problématiques"Which, in my opinion, could address several of these issues." Three critical elements here: (1) "À mon sens" (in my view) — a parenthetical hedging phrase that prevents the writer from sounding like they are dictating to the mayor. It signals: "This is my perspective, not an absolute truth." (2) "Pourrait" — conditional of pouvoir, expressing possibility rather than certainty. The proposal could work — the writer does not claim it will work. (3) "Problématiques" — more formal and analytical than "problèmes." It implies systemic issues, not just isolated complaints.Grammar — conditional for possibility; Vocabulary — à mon sens (hedging), problématiques (analytical term); Sociolinguistic — modesty and respect for the mayor's judgment
: la création d'un jardin partagé."The creation of a community garden." The colon introduces the proposal dramatically. After building up ("I have a proposal that could address these issues..."), the colon delivers it. This is a rhetorical technique — the reader expects something after the buildup, and the colon satisfies that expectation. "Jardin partagé" is the standard French term for a community garden — a specific, recognized concept in French urban planning.Coherence — rhetorical delivery; Vocabulary — jardin partagé (domain-specific term)

Strategic function of Sentence 4: This sentence is the pivot of the entire letter — the moment where the description of problems becomes a proposal for solutions. The sentence is carefully calibrated to be neither too bold nor too timid: the conditional (pourrait), the hedging (à mon sens), and the respectful submission (soumettre) all signal that the writer respects the mayor's authority to accept or reject the idea.

Sentence 8

Il conviendrait, dans un premier temps, de consulter les associations de quartier afin de recueillir les avis des résidents.

SegmentAnnotationTEF Criterion
Il conviendrait,Impersonal conditional construction. "It would be appropriate to." "Convenir" (to be appropriate/suitable) in the impersonal conditional (il conviendrait) is one of the most powerful suggestion structures in formal French (Ch. 22). 🔟 THE PERSUASION ARCHITECTURE — UNIQUE TO SUGGESTION LETTERS

The suggestion letter follows a persuasion sequence that differs from both the complaint (evidence-based) and the request (deference-based):

The Suggestion Letter Persuasion Sequence

STEP 1 — ESTABLISH STANDING (P1, Sentence 1)
"I am a long-term resident and many people share my concern."
→ The mayor thinks: "This is a legitimate community voice."

STEP 2 — DESCRIBE THE PROBLEM WITH SPECIFICS (P1, Sentences 2-3)
"Here are the specific deficiencies: poor maintenance,
dilapidated playgrounds, nothing for the elderly."
→ The mayor thinks: "These are real, concrete issues."

STEP 3 — TRANSITION TO SOLUTION (P2, Sentence 4)
"I have a proposal that could address several of these issues."
→ The mayor thinks: "This citizen is constructive, not just complaining."

STEP 4 — PRESENT THE PROPOSAL (P2, Sentence 4)
"A community garden."
→ The mayor thinks: "Interesting. What are the benefits?"

STEP 5 — LIST MULTIPLE BENEFITS (P2, Sentences 5-6)
"Intergenerational space, social bonds, beautification,
environmental education, elderly activity."
→ The mayor thinks: "This addresses multiple constituencies.
Politically, this could be popular."

STEP 6 — PROPOSE CONCRETE IMPLEMENTATION (P3, Sentences 7-8)
"This specific location. Consult associations first."
→ The mayor thinks: "This person has thought it through.
The consultation approach is democratic."

STEP 7 — OFFER PERSONAL COMMITMENT (P3, Sentence 9)
"I will help organize and coordinate volunteers."
→ The mayor thinks: "This won't require only municipal resources.
Citizens are willing to contribute."

STEP 8 — INVITE DIALOGUE (Pre-closing, Sentence 10)
"I would be delighted to discuss this further."
→ The mayor thinks: "This is a citizen I could work with."

The Key Persuasion Principle: Problem → Solution → Implementation → Commitment

This four-part sequence is the structural formula for civic proposals. Each part answers a question the recipient is asking:

Recipient's QuestionYour AnswerWhere
"Why are you writing to me?""Because there is a problem in our community."P1
"What do you suggest?""A community garden with these five benefits."P2
"How would this work practically?""This location, this process, this timeline."P3
"Why should I invest my time in this?""Because I and other residents will contribute personally."P3 + Pre-closing

The most powerful element is Step 7 — personal commitment. A proposal that says "the city should do X" places the entire burden on the recipient. A proposal that says "the city should do X, and I will personally help" shares the burden — making it far more likely to receive a positive response.

1️⃣1️⃣ WHAT COULD GO WRONG — SUGGESTION-SPECIFIC PITFALLS

Pitfall 1: Sounding Like a Complaint Instead of a Suggestion

❌ Notre quartier est dans un état lamentable. La mairie ne fait rien pour entretenir le parc. C'est inacceptable. Vous devez agir immédiatement.

Why it fails: This is a complaint disguised as a suggestion. The tone is accusatory ("la mairie ne fait rien"), the language is aggressive ("inacceptable"), and the demand is direct ("vous devez agir"). A suggestion letter must be constructive, not combative.

The fix (from this chapter): Describe the problems objectively ("le parc souffre d'un entretien insuffisant"), then pivot to a proposal ("face à cette situation, je souhaiterais vous soumettre une proposition"). The same information is communicated — the park is poorly maintained — but the tone is observation, not accusation.

Pitfall 2: Being Too Vague

❌ Il faudrait faire quelque chose pour améliorer notre quartier. Peut-être un jardin ou un espace vert. Ce serait bien.

Why it fails: No specific proposal, no concrete location, no implementation details, no benefits articulated. The prompt requires "des modalités concrètes de mise en œuvre" — this response offers none.

The fix (from this chapter): Name the specific proposal (community garden), identify a specific location (rue Matabiau), describe specific benefits (five listed), and propose specific implementation steps (community consultation first).

Pitfall 3: Overstepping Your Authority

❌ Je vous demande d'allouer un budget de 50 000 euros à ce projet et de commencer les travaux avant l'été. Vous devriez également nommer un responsable municipal pour coordonner l'initiative.

Why it fails: The citizen is giving orders to the mayor — allocating budgets, setting deadlines, assigning staff. This exceeds the writer's role. A citizen can suggest, propose, and recommend. A citizen cannot demand, instruct, or assign.

The fix (from this chapter): Use impersonal constructions ("il conviendrait de") and conditional mood ("me semblerait constituer") to propose ideas without issuing directives. Offer your own participation rather than dictating what the municipality should do.

Pitfall 4: Forgetting to Offer Your Own Participation

❌ Je vous propose de créer un jardin partagé. J'espère que la mairie pourra mettre ce projet en place rapidement.

Why it fails: The prompt specifically requires "proposer votre participation." Beyond the prompt requirement, a proposal without personal commitment is weak — it says "you should do this" without "and I will help." The recipient has no reason to prioritize a suggestion from someone who is not willing to invest their own time.

The fix (from this chapter): "Je me tiens personnellement prête à participer à l'organisation de ce projet et à coordonner un groupe de bénévoles." Specific role, personal commitment, concrete contribution.

Pitfall 5: Using "On" Instead of Formal Impersonal Constructions

❌ On pourrait faire un jardin. On devrait demander aux gens ce qu'ils en pensent.

Why it fails: "On" is the informal impersonal pronoun — appropriate for spoken French and informal writing, but too casual for a formal letter to a mayor. The formal equivalent is the impersonal construction with "il":

Informal (on)Formal (impersonal)
On pourrait faire un jardin.Il serait envisageable de créer un jardin.
On devrait demander aux gens.Il conviendrait de consulter les résidents.
On pourrait utiliser le terrain.Le terrain... me semblerait constituer un emplacement idéal.

💡 The impersonal construction is the suggestion letter's formal signature. Just as the complaint letter uses the passive voice and the request letter uses the conditional for politeness, the suggestion letter uses impersonal constructions (il conviendrait, il serait souhaitable, il semblerait) to propose actions without attributing them to any specific person. Mastering these constructions is essential for a B2 suggestion letter.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY — WHAT THIS CHAPTER TEACHES THAT NO OTHER CHAPTER COVERS

Chapter 39 is the only chapter that demonstrates the civic register, the impersonal construction system, and the B1 vs. B2 comparison in a complete letter.

The key lessons unique to this chapter:

  1. The suggestion letter requires a collective voice. Using "nous" and "notre" transforms a personal opinion into a community concern — and community concerns carry more weight with public officials.

  2. Impersonal constructions are the grammar of civic proposals. "Il conviendrait de" proposes an action without commanding anyone to perform it. This grammatical modesty is essential when writing to someone who has the power to say no.

  3. The conditional reaches its maximum density in suggestion letters. Nine conditional forms in 260 words — nearly one per sentence. Every benefit is hypothetical, every proposal is tentative, every offer is softened.

  4. The B1-B2 gap is not about ideas — it is about execution. The B1 and B2 versions of this letter contain identical ideas, identical problems, and identical solutions. The difference is entirely in vocabulary, grammar, structure, and conventions. A student who reads both versions side by side can see exactly what they need to change to move from B1 to B2.

  5. Civic vocabulary is a distinct register. Words like lien social, sensibiliser, embellissement, mise en œuvre, aménagement, bénévoles belong to civic discourse. They signal that the writer engages with community life and public policy — a key competence for someone living in a Francophone society.

  6. The persuasion architecture of a suggestion is Problem → Solution → Implementation → Commitment. Each part answers a specific question the recipient is asking. Missing any part weakens the proposal.

  7. Personal commitment is the strongest persuasion tool. A proposal that says "I will help" is fundamentally more compelling than a proposal that says "you should act." Sentence 9's offer of personal participation transforms the letter from a wish list into a partnership invitation.
