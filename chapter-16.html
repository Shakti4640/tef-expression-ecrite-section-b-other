📖 THE EXAM MOMENT

You open the exam booklet. The prompt reads:

Situation :
Vous habitez dans un quartier résidentiel depuis plusieurs années. Récemment, vous avez remarqué que les espaces verts du quartier sont mal entretenus, que l'éclairage public est insuffisant le soir et que les familles manquent d'aires de jeux pour les enfants.

Consigne :
Vous écrivez une lettre au maire de votre ville pour lui proposer des améliorations concernant votre quartier. Dans votre lettre, vous devez :

  • vous présenter et expliquer votre attachement au quartier ;
  • décrire les problèmes que vous avez constatés ;
  • proposer des solutions concrètes.

Rédigez une lettre formelle d'au moins 200 mots.

You read the prompt twice, as you learned in Ch. 5. You extract the four elements:

  • WHO you are: A long-time resident of a residential neighbourhood
  • WHO you write to: The mayor of your city
  • WHY: To propose improvements for your neighbourhood
  • WHAT to include: Self-introduction and attachment, problems observed, concrete solutions

You have already written the header (Ch. 6–8), the Objet line (Ch. 9), the salutation — Monsieur le Maire, (Ch. 10) — and the first sentence hook (Ch. 11).

Now you must write Paragraph 1. And here is where the suggestion letter diverges fundamentally from everything you learned in Ch. 14 (complaints) and Ch. 15 (requests).

1️⃣ WHAT IS THE CHALLENGE, IN PLAIN LANGUAGE?

In a complaint letter, your standing is automatic: you are a wronged customer. The company owes you something. The power dynamic is clear.

In a request letter, your standing is functional: you need something, and you explain why the recipient is positioned to provide it.

In a suggestion letter, you have no automatic standing. Nobody asked for your opinion. Nobody owes you a platform. The mayor does not wake up in the morning hoping to receive letters from residents telling them what to fix.

This creates a unique rhetorical challenge: you must earn the right to be heard before you say anything.

Paragraph 1 of a suggestion letter is not about the problem. It is not about your solution. It is about you — specifically, about establishing why your voice on this topic deserves the recipient's attention.

This is what makes the suggestion letter the most rhetorically sophisticated of all six letter types. And it is why many candidates fail it: they jump straight into describing problems (which sounds like a complaint) or proposing ideas (which sounds presumptuous) without first positioning themselves as a credible, constructive voice.

2️⃣ WHY PARAGRAPH 1 MATTERS SPECIFICALLY IN SUGGESTION LETTERS

The Examiner's Checkpoint

When the examiner reads P1 of a suggestion letter, they are asking one question:

"Has this candidate established a legitimate voice before proposing anything?"

If the answer is no — if you dive straight into "The parks are terrible and you should fix them" — the examiner registers a sociolinguistic failure. Not because your French is wrong, but because you have not understood the social dynamics of writing to an authority figure with unsolicited suggestions.

The French Cultural Dimension

In French administrative and civic culture, there is a deeply embedded convention: before you suggest, you position. This means:

  1. You establish your connection to the issue (why it concerns you personally)
  2. You acknowledge the positive aspects of the current situation (you are not attacking)
  3. You signal that your intention is constructive, not adversarial

This convention exists because French formal communication distinguishes sharply between:

  • Une réclamation (a complaint — something went wrong, you want correction)
  • Une proposition (a suggestion — things could be better, you have ideas)

Mixing the two is one of the most common errors in TEF suggestion letters. The candidate thinks they are suggesting but writes as if they are complaining. Paragraph 1 is where this distinction must be established beyond any doubt.

The Tone Contract

Chapter 13 established that P1 sets the tone contract for the entire letter. In a suggestion letter, the tone contract is:

"I am a concerned, engaged, and constructive member of this community. I am writing not to criticize, but to contribute. I respect your role and I believe improvement is possible."

If P1 establishes this contract successfully, the examiner reads P2 (where you describe problems) and P3 (where you propose solutions) through a lens of goodwill. If P1 fails to establish this contract, the same P2 and P3 read as presumptuous, critical, or naive.

3️⃣ THE "OBSERVATION → CONCERN → PROPOSAL PREVIEW" STRUCTURE

Unlike the complaint P1 structure (reference → problem → impact, Ch. 14) or the request P1 structure (positioning → need identification → legitimacy, Ch. 15), the suggestion letter P1 follows a three-beat structure unique to its type:

Beat 1: OBSERVATION — Establish Who You Are and What You Have Observed

This is where you state your identity in relation to the issue. You are not just "a person." You are a resident, a parent, an employee, a member — someone with a specific vantage point that makes your observations credible.

Beat 2: CONCERN — Express Constructive Concern (Not Complaint)

This is where you acknowledge that something could be improved. The key word is could — not "must," not "should have been," not "is terrible." You frame the issue as an opportunity, not a failure.

Beat 3: PROPOSAL PREVIEW — Signal That You Have Ideas

This is where you announce — without detail — that you have constructive suggestions. This serves as a paragraph-level transition to P2, and it tells the examiner: "This candidate knows where they are going."

The Three Beats in Action

Here is how the three beats appear in a single P1, using the exam prompt from the opening of this chapter:

Habitant du quartier des Tilleuls depuis plus de huit ans, je suis particulièrement attaché à notre cadre de vie et à la qualité de notre environnement quotidien. [Beat 1: identity + connection] Cependant, au cours des derniers mois, j'ai pu constater que certains aspects de notre quartier mériteraient d'être améliorés, notamment en ce qui concerne l'entretien des espaces verts et l'éclairage public. [Beat 2: constructive concern] C'est pourquoi je me permets de vous soumettre quelques propositions qui pourraient, je l'espère, contribuer à l'amélioration de notre cadre de vie. [Beat 3: proposal preview]

Word count: approximately 90 words — slightly above the 40–55 word target from Ch. 13. This is acceptable for suggestion letters because the positioning work requires more space. You can compensate by keeping P2 or P3 slightly shorter.

4️⃣ ESTABLISHING YOUR STANDING — THE "EN TANT QUE" SYSTEM

Why Standing Matters

In formal French, you cannot simply say "I think the park should be better." The implicit question from any authority figure is: "Et vous êtes qui, exactement?" — "And who exactly are you?"

Your standing answers this question. It tells the recipient: "I am not a random person. I am someone whose perspective on this topic is grounded in direct experience."

The "En tant que" Construction

The most powerful and versatile structure for establishing standing in French formal writing is "en tant que" (in my capacity as / as a):

Structure: En tant que + [your role], je + [verb]

This construction does three things simultaneously:

  1. Identifies your role
  2. Legitimizes your right to write
  3. Signals formal register (this construction is inherently formal)

5️⃣ THE CONCERN EXPRESSION — FORMAL OBSERVATION WITHOUT COMPLAINT

The Linguistic Tightrope

This is the most delicate part of the suggestion letter P1. You must communicate that something is not ideal without sounding like you are complaining. The difference is subtle but critical:

Complaint RegisterSuggestion Register
Le parc est dans un état lamentable. (The park is in a deplorable state.)L'entretien du parc mériterait d'être renforcé. (The park's maintenance could benefit from being strengthened.)
L'éclairage est totalement insuffisant. (The lighting is totally insufficient.)L'éclairage public pourrait être amélioré. (The public lighting could be improved.)
Rien n'a été fait pour les enfants. (Nothing has been done for children.)Les familles du quartier bénéficieraient d'espaces de jeux supplémentaires. (The neighbourhood families would benefit from additional play areas.)
C'est inacceptable. (This is unacceptable.)Il me semble que des améliorations seraient souhaitables. (It seems to me that improvements would be desirable.)

The pattern: Complaint language uses absolute judgments (lamentable, totalement, rien, inacceptable). Suggestion language uses conditional possibility (mériterait, pourrait, bénéficieraient, seraient souhaitables).

The Conditional as a Diplomatic Tool

The conditional tense (le conditionnel présent) is the primary grammatical weapon of the suggestion letter. In P1, it appears specifically in concern expressions:

VerbConditional FormMeaning in ContextExample

pouvoirpourrait / pourraient"could"L'éclairage public pourrait être renforcé.
bénéficierbénéficierait / bénéficieraient"would benefit from"Les résidents bénéficieraient d'un meilleur accès aux transports.

One grammatical choice, two scoring criteria satisfied.

6️⃣ THE PROPOSAL PREVIEW — SIGNALING WHAT COMES NEXT

Why You Need a Proposal Preview in P1

Chapter 13 established that P1 must create a logical bridge to P2. In a suggestion letter, this bridge is the proposal preview — a sentence that announces you have ideas, without detailing them.

The proposal preview serves three functions:

  1. For the examiner: It proves you have a plan. Your letter is going somewhere. This scores under Cohérence et cohésion.
  2. For the fictional recipient: It creates anticipation. The mayor now knows that after the context, concrete proposals are coming.
  3. For you as a writer: It commits you to a direction. Once you've announced proposals, you must deliver them in P2/P3. This prevents the common error of describing problems endlessly without ever suggesting solutions.

The "Je me permets de" Family

You will have noticed that "je me permets de" appears in nearly every proposal preview. This is not repetition — it is a core structure of French formal suggestion writing. Let us examine it deeply.

What "je me permets de" literally means:
"I permit myself to" — or more naturally, "I take the liberty of."

What it signals:
It signals that you are aware you are making an unsolicited approach. You acknowledge that the recipient did not ask for your letter. By saying "I take the liberty," you show deference — you recognize that writing to this person is a liberty you are taking, not a right you are exercising.

Why it matters for TEF:
This single expression simultaneously satisfies:

  • Adaptation sociolinguistique — you understand the social dynamics of unsolicited formal correspondence
  • Étendue du vocabulaire — you use a pronominal construction (se permettre de) correctly
  • Correction grammaticale — the structure requires correct pronoun placement and infinitive complement

Variations of "je me permets de":

VariationFormality LevelUsage Note
Je me permets de vous écrire pour...Standard formalMost common, always safe
Je me permets de vous adresser cette lettre afin de...Higher formal"Adresser cette lettre" is more formal than "écrire"
Aussi me permets-je de...Very formal (literary)Subject-verb inversion after "aussi" at sentence start — C1 marker. Use only if you are confident with this structure.
Je prends la liberté de vous écrire...FormalAlternative phrasing — same meaning, slightly different style
Permettez-moi de...Formal (imperative)Slightly more direct — you are asking permission rather than taking it. Works well but less humble than "je me permets."

⚠️ What to NEVER do: Do not write "Je me permet" (without the final -s). This is a conjugation error (se permettre with je → je me permets). It is one of the most common spelling errors in TEF formal writing and it occurs in a high-visibility position. The examiner sees it immediately.

7️⃣ COMPLETE P1 MODELS — BY RECIPIENT TYPE

Model A: Suggestion to a Mayor (Neighbourhood Improvement)

Prompt context: You want to propose improvements to your neighbourhood's public spaces.

Habitant du quartier des Tilleuls depuis plus de huit ans, je suis particulièrement attaché à notre cadre de vie. Cependant, au fil des derniers mois, j'ai pu constater que certains aspects de notre environnement quotidien mériteraient d'être améliorés, notamment en ce qui concerne l'entretien des espaces verts et l'éclairage public. C'est pourquoi je me permets de vous soumettre quelques propositions qui pourraient, à mon sens, contribuer au bien-être de l'ensemble des résidents.

Annotation:

  • Beat 1: Habitant du quartier... depuis plus de huit ans → standing + duration (appositive construction)
  • Attachment marker: je suis particulièrement attaché à notre cadre de vie → emotional connection to the place — signals genuine concern, not random criticism
  • Beat 2: Cependant → concessive connector (Ch. 25); j'ai pu constater que → formal observation; mériteraient d'être améliorés → conditional for diplomatic concern; notamment en ce qui concerne → specifying the areas
  • Beat 3: C'est pourquoi → causal connector; je me permets de vous soumettre → deference; qui pourraient contribuer → conditional for proposal
  • Word count: ~75 words — appropriate for a suggestion letter P1

Model B: Suggestion to a School Director (Extracurricular Activities)

Prompt context: You want to propose new extracurricular activities at your child's school.

En tant que parent d'un élève de CM2, je suis avec attention la vie scolaire de l'école Jules-Ferry et j'apprécie la qualité de l'enseignement qui y est dispensé. Toutefois, il me semble que l'offre d'activités périscolaires pourrait être enrichie afin de répondre aux besoins variés des élèves. Je souhaiterais, par la présente, vous faire part de quelques suggestions à ce sujet.

Annotation:

  • Beat 1: En tant que parent d'un élève de CM2 → standing with precision (class level specified)
  • Positive acknowledgment: j'apprécie la qualité de l'enseignement → concession before concern — extremely effective
  • Beat 2: Toutefois → opposition connector; il me semble que → softened observation; pourrait être enrichie → conditional + positive framing ("enriched" not "fixed")
  • Beat 3: Je souhaiterais, par la présente → conditional politeness + formal marker (par la présente)
  • Word count: ~65 words

Model C: Suggestion to an Employer (Workplace Improvement)

Prompt context: You want to propose a new work-from-home policy at your company.

Employé au sein du service marketing depuis trois ans, j'ai toujours apprécié l'esprit d'innovation qui caractérise notre entreprise. Néanmoins, à la suite de plusieurs échanges avec mes collègues, j'ai constaté qu'un certain nombre d'entre nous souhaiteraient bénéficier d'une plus grande flexibilité dans l'organisation de notre travail. Je me permets donc de vous écrire afin de vous soumettre une proposition relative à la mise en place du télétravail partiel.

Annotation:

  • Beat 1: Employé au sein du service marketing depuis trois ans → standing + department + duration; j'ai toujours apprécié → positive acknowledgment of company culture
  • Beat 2: Néanmoins → opposition connector; à la suite de plusieurs échanges avec mes collègues → shows the concern is shared, not individual; souhaiteraient bénéficier → conditional for collective desire
  • Beat 3: Je me permets donc de vous écrire → deference; une proposition relative à la mise en place du télétravail partiel → precise preview of what will be proposed
  • Word count: ~80 words

Model D: Suggestion to a Newspaper Editor (Civic Initiative)

Prompt context: You want to suggest that the newspaper cover local environmental initiatives more prominently.

Lecteur assidu de votre quotidien depuis de nombreuses années, j'apprécie la richesse et la diversité des sujets que vous abordez. Cependant, j'ai remarqué que les initiatives environnementales locales, pourtant nombreuses dans notre région, bénéficient d'une couverture médiatique relativement limitée. Convaincu que votre journal pourrait jouer un rôle déterminant dans la sensibilisation des citoyens à ces enjeux, je me permets de vous adresser cette lettre afin de vous soumettre une suggestion.

Annotation:

  • Beat 1: Lecteur assidu... depuis de nombreuses années → standing as loyal reader; j'apprécie la richesse et la diversité → positive acknowledgment
  • Beat 2: Cependant → concession; j'ai remarqué que → observation; pourtant nombreuses → parenthetical addition showing knowledge; bénéficient d'une couverture médiatique relativement limitée → diplomatic understatement ("relativement limitée" not "insuffisante")
  • Beat 3: Convaincu que votre journal pourrait jouer un rôle déterminant → flattery through capability attribution; je me permets de vous adresser cette lettre → deference
  • Word count: ~85 words
  • Level marker: This P1 approaches C1 with its participial construction (Convaincu que), parenthetical insertion (pourtant nombreuses), and sophisticated vocabulary (couverture médiatique, sensibilisation, enjeux)

8️⃣ GRAMMAR FOCUS: PRESENT TENSE FOR DESCRIBING CURRENT SITUATIONS

Why Present Tense Dominates Suggestion Letter P1

In a complaint letter (Ch. 14), the passé composé dominates P1 because you are narrating past events.

In a suggestion letter, the present tense (indicatif présent) dominates P1 because you are describing ongoing conditions — things that are true now and have been true for some time.

TenseRole in Suggestion P1Example
Présent de l'indicatifDescribes current state of affairsLes espaces verts sont mal entretenus.
Présent de l'indicatif (habitual)Describes recurring situationsLes familles manquent d'aires de jeux.
Passé composéReports your observation (the act of noticing)J'ai constaté que certains aspects mériteraient...
Conditionnel présentSoftens judgments about what could be betterL'éclairage pourrait être amélioré.
Présent with depuisEstablishes your duration of connectionJ'habite ce quartier depuis huit ans.

The "Depuis + Present Tense" Rule

This is a grammar point that English speakers consistently get wrong, and getting it right in P1 scores immediate points:

In English: "I have lived here for eight years." → Present perfect
In French: "J'habite ici depuis huit ans." → Present tense

French uses the present tense with depuis for actions that started in the past and continue in the present. This is not optional — using the passé composé ("J'ai habité ici depuis huit ans") is a grammatical error.

Correct (Present + depuis)Incorrect (Passé composé + depuis)
Je travaille dans cette entreprise depuis trois ans. ✅J'ai travaillé dans cette entreprise depuis trois ans. ❌
Nous habitons ce quartier depuis 2015. ✅Nous avons habité ce quartier depuis 2015. ❌
Je suis membre de cette association depuis cinq ans. ✅J'ai été membre de cette association depuis cinq ans. ❌

💡 TEF scoring impact: The depuis + present rule is tested across all TEF sections. Getting it right in your letter's opening paragraph — where it naturally appears — demonstrates grammatical competence in a high-visibility position.

The Participial Appositive Construction

Several model P1s above use a construction that deserves explicit grammar attention:

Structure: [Present participle or past participle phrase], je...

Examples:

  • Habitant du quartier depuis huit ans, je suis attaché à...
  • Résidant dans cette commune depuis longtemps, je me permets de...
  • Convaincu de l'importance de ce sujet, je souhaiterais...

Grammar rules:

  1. The participle must agree with the subject of the main clause (je):
    • Male writer: Habitant, Convaincu, Employé
    • Female writer: Habitante, Convaincue, Employée
  2. The participle phrase is separated by a comma from the main clause
  3. The subject of the participle must be the same as the subject of the main clause (dangling participle error if not)

Dangling participle error (what NOT to do):

❌ Habitant du quartier depuis huit ans, les espaces verts sont mal entretenus.

This says the green spaces have been living in the neighbourhood for eight years. The participle "Habitant" must refer to the subject of the main clause, which is "les espaces verts" — nonsensical.

✅ Habitant du quartier depuis huit ans, je constate que les espaces verts sont mal entretenus.

Now "Habitant" refers to "je" — correct.

9️⃣ WHAT COULD GO WRONG — SUGGESTION P1 SPECIFIC ERRORS

Error 1: Writing a Complaint Disguised as a Suggestion

What happens: The candidate describes problems with aggressive, absolute language, then adds "I have suggestions" at the end. The examiner reads it as a complaint with a thin suggestion veneer.

❌ Je suis vraiment mécontent de l'état déplorable de notre quartier. Rien n'est fait pour améliorer les choses. Les espaces verts sont abandonnés et l'éclairage est catastrophique. J'ai quand même quelques suggestions.

Why it's wrong: "Vraiment mécontent," "état déplorable," "rien n'est fait," "abandonnés," "catastrophique" — this is complaint vocabulary. "J'ai quand même quelques suggestions" — "quand même" (informal register) + the entire tone is adversarial.

✅ Résidant dans notre quartier depuis plusieurs années, je suis attaché à son cadre de vie. Toutefois, j'ai pu constater que certains espaces publics mériteraient un entretien plus régulier et que l'éclairage pourrait être renforcé. C'est dans un esprit constructif que je me permets de vous soumettre quelques propositions.

What changed: Every negative is reframed as an opportunity. "Mériteraient" replaces "sont abandonnés." "Pourrait être renforcé" replaces "est catastrophique." "Dans un esprit constructif" explicitly signals the tone.

Error 2: Failing to Establish Standing

What happens: The candidate jumps directly into the problem without saying who they are or why they care.

❌ Les espaces verts de notre quartier sont mal entretenus. L'éclairage public est insuffisant. Les enfants n'ont pas d'aires de jeux. Je voudrais proposer des améliorations.

Why it's wrong: Who is writing this? A resident of one month? A visitor? A real estate developer? Without standing, the observations have no weight and the proposal has no legitimacy.

✅ Habitant du quartier des Tilleuls depuis plus de huit ans, j'ai pu observer au fil du temps l'évolution de notre cadre de vie. Si de nombreux aspects demeurent agréables, il me semble toutefois que certains points, tels que l'entretien des espaces verts et l'éclairage public, mériteraient une attention renouvelée.

What changed: Standing established immediately (Habitant... depuis plus de huit ans). Duration adds credibility. Positive acknowledgment (de nombreux aspects demeurent agréables) prevents complaint tone.

Error 3: Being Too Vague in the Proposal Preview

What happens: The candidate says they want to "share some thoughts" but gives no indication of what those thoughts concern.

❌ Je vous écris pour partager quelques idées que j'ai eues.

Why it's wrong: This is vague and informal. "Des idées que j'ai eues" sounds like a casual conversation. The examiner has no idea what the letter will be about.

✅ Je me permets de vous écrire afin de vous soumettre quelques propositions relatives à l'aménagement des espaces publics de notre quartier.

What changed: "Propositions relatives à l'aménagement des espaces publics" — the reader now knows exactly what domain the proposals will address. "Soumettre" replaces "partager" (more formal, more humble). "Afin de" replaces an implicit purpose (Ch. 25 connector).

Error 4: Excessive Flattery

What happens: The candidate spends too many words praising the recipient before getting to the point.

❌ Monsieur le Maire, je tiens tout d'abord à vous féliciter pour l'excellent travail que vous accomplissez pour notre belle ville. Grâce à vous, notre commune est devenue un endroit merveilleux où il fait bon vivre. Vos efforts sont remarquables et nous vous en sommes tous reconnaissants. Cependant, je voudrais quand même vous dire que...

Why it's wrong: Four sentences of flattery before any substance. The examiner sees through this immediately. It wastes word count. And "quand même" at the end undermines everything — it signals that the flattery was insincere padding.

The rule: One sentence of positive acknowledgment is enough. Two is acceptable. Three or more is excessive and counterproductive.

Error 5: Using the Wrong Connector Between Concern and Proposal Preview

Wrong ChoiceWhy It's WrongRight Choice
Donc, je voudrais proposer..."Donc" at sentence start is informal. Too abrupt.C'est pourquoi je me permets de proposer...
Alors, j'ai des suggestions."Alors" is oral register. "J'ai des suggestions" is flat and informal.Je souhaiterais donc vous soumettre quelques propositions.
Du coup, je vous écris."Du coup" is strictly informal/oral. Absolute prohibition in formal writing.C'est dans cette perspective que je me permets de vous écrire.
Par contre, j'ai des idées."Par contre" is debated but generally avoided in formal writing. And the sentence is too simple.Néanmoins, je me permets de vous adresser quelques suggestions.

🔟 B1 vs. B2 vs. C1 — THE SAME SUGGESTION P1 AT THREE LEVELS

The Prompt

You are a resident who wants to suggest to the mayor that the local market be held twice a week instead of once.

B1 Level

Bonjour Monsieur le Maire. J'habite dans votre ville et je vais souvent au marché le samedi. Je pense que le marché pourrait être deux fois par semaine. Je vais vous expliquer pourquoi.

Diagnosis:

  • ❌ "Bonjour" — informal salutation for a letter to the mayor
  • ❌ No standing established (how long have you lived here? what is your connection?)
  • ❌ No positive acknowledgment
  • ❌ "Je pense que le marché pourrait être deux fois par semaine" — jumps directly to the proposal without context or concern phase
  • ❌ "Je vais vous expliquer pourquoi" — oral register, too direct
  • ❌ No formal vocabulary, no connectors, simple sentence structures
  • ❌ No Objet line, no header referenced

B2 Level

Résidant dans la commune de Villeneuve depuis plus de six ans, je fréquente régulièrement le marché hebdomadaire qui se tient chaque samedi sur la place centrale. Ce marché constitue, à mes yeux, un élément essentiel de la vie locale. Cependant, j'ai constaté que de nombreux habitants regrettent de ne pouvoir en profiter qu'une seule fois par semaine. C'est pourquoi je me permets de vous écrire afin de vous soumettre une proposition visant à élargir la fréquence de ce marché.

Diagnosis:

  • ✅ Standing established with duration (depuis plus de six ans)
  • ✅ Participial appositive construction (Résidant dans...)
  • ✅ Positive acknowledgment (un élément essentiel de la vie locale)
  • ✅ Concern expressed diplomatically (de nombreux habitants regrettent de ne pouvoir en profiter qu'une seule fois)
  • ✅ Proposal preview with precise subject (élargir la fréquence de ce marché)
  • ✅ Formal connectors (Cependant, C'est pourquoi)
  • ✅ "Je me permets de" — deference
  • ✅ Complex grammar: ne...que (restrictive), present participle, infinitive complements
  • ✅ Approximately 80 words — appropriate length

C1 Level

Fidèle habitant de la commune de Villeneuve depuis bientôt une décennie, c'est en tant qu'usager assidu du marché hebdomadaire — rendez-vous incontournable de notre vie de quartier — que je me permets de solliciter votre attention. Tout en saluant le dynamisme de cette tradition qui rassemble commerçants locaux et résidents chaque samedi matin, force est de constater que la demande croissante des habitants dépasse désormais l'offre qu'une seule journée de marché peut satisfaire. C'est dans cette perspective que je souhaiterais vous soumettre une proposition susceptible de répondre à cette attente collective.

Diagnosis:

  • ✅ Everything from B2, plus:
  • ✅ Appositive parenthetical (— rendez-vous incontournable de notre vie de quartier —)
  • ✅ "Force est de constater que" — C1-level impersonal observation
  • ✅ "Tout en saluant" — gérondif with concessive value
  • ✅ Sophisticated vocabulary: décennie, solliciter votre attention, dynamisme, croissante, susceptible de
  • ✅ Rhetorical rhythm: longer sentences with embedded clauses, balanced structure
  • ✅ The concern is framed entirely as a supply-demand analysis, not as a complaint
  • ✅ Approximately 90 words

1️⃣1️⃣ WHAT TO NEVER DO IN A SUGGESTION LETTER P1

These are absolute prohibitions — not just weak choices, but errors that fundamentally misrepresent the letter type:

❌ Never Do ThisWhy It's Prohibited
Use the word "exiger" (to demand)You are suggesting, not demanding. "Exiger" belongs in complaint letters (Ch. 26).
Use "je suis mécontent(e)" or "je suis déçu(e)"These are complaint emotions. Suggestion letters express concern, not personal dissatisfaction. Use "je souhaiterais attirer votre attention sur" instead.
Use "vous devez" / "vous devriez"Telling the recipient what they "must" or "should" do is presumptuous. Use "il serait souhaitable de" or "il me semble que... mériterait."
Start P1 with the problem directlyAlways establish standing first. The problem comes second. Starting with the problem makes it a complaint.
Use "du coup," "en fait," "en gros," "genre"Strictly oral/informal register. Absolute prohibition in any formal letter. (Ch. 3 register rules.)
Write more than 3 sentences of positive acknowledgmentExcessive flattery wastes word count and sounds insincere. One to two sentences maximum.
Use the passé composé for the current situation you want to improveThe situation is ongoing → present tense. "Les espaces verts sont mal entretenus" (present) not "Les espaces verts ont été mal entretenus" (passé composé — implies it happened once and ended).
Omit the proposal preview entirelyWithout it, P1 is just context with no forward momentum. The examiner doesn't know what's coming. Cohesion score drops.

1️⃣2️⃣ SPECIAL CASE: WHEN THE PROMPT SPECIFIES "SEMI-FORMAL"

Some suggestion prompts may indicate a semi-formal context — for example, writing to a local association president you know personally, or to a colleague rather than a director. In these cases, the suggestion P1 adjusts:

💡 Critical point: Even in semi-formal, you maintain: proper salutation, Objet line, structured paragraphs, and a complete closing formula. Semi-formal means the vocabulary and tone soften, not that the structure disappears. (Ch. 3 register rules apply.)

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY — WHAT THIS CHAPTER TEACHES THAT NO OTHER CHAPTER COVERS

Chapter 16 is the only chapter that teaches you how to earn the right to be heard.

In every other letter type, your standing is given by the situation: you are a wronged customer (complaint), a person with a need (request), a job applicant (cover letter). In a suggestion letter, you must construct your own legitimacy through the three-beat structure: standing → constructive concern → proposal preview.

The core principle: In French formal writing, you do not suggest improvements to someone more powerful than you without first demonstrating that your voice is informed, your tone is constructive, and your intention is collaborative. Paragraph 1 is where all three of these things are established.

The grammatical signature of the suggestion P1 is the conditional tense for diplomatic observation (mériterait, pourrait, gagnerait), the present tense with depuis for standing, and the "je me permets de" construction for deference.

Master these three elements and your suggestion letter P1 will immediately read as B2.
