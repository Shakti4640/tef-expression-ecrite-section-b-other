📖 THE EXAM MOMENT

You are writing Paragraph 3. You know what you want to say — you want to request a refund, propose an alternative, express availability for an interview, or suggest a policy change. The ideas are clear in your mind.

But the moment your pen touches paper, you face a series of rapid-fire grammar decisions:

  • "Should this be je souhaiterais or je souhaite?"
  • "Is it si vous pourriez or si vous pouviez?"
  • "Does je demande que take the subjunctive?"
  • "What is the conditional of savoir?"
  • "Can I write bien que with the indicative?"

Each decision takes a fraction of a second — but each wrong decision costs points. A single si vous pourriez in an otherwise perfect letter is like a stain on a white shirt: the examiner cannot unsee it.

This chapter gives you complete mastery of the three grammar systems that dominate P3 across all letter types. It is not a grammar textbook — it is a combat manual for the specific structures you will need under exam pressure.

1️⃣ THE THREE GRAMMAR SYSTEMS OF P3

Why These Three?

Paragraph 3 — across all six letter types — relies on three grammar systems more than any other part of the letter:

SystemWhat It Does in P3Where It Appears
1. The ConditionalSoftens demands, expresses wishes, presents possibilities, frames proposalsEvery P3 type — the dominant mood
2. The SubjunctiveExpresses necessity, doubt, purpose, and emotion after specific triggersComplaint P3 (je demande que), suggestion P3 (il est essentiel que), opinion P3 (il faudrait que, pour que)
3. The Si-Clause SystemCreates diplomatic conditions, hypothetical scenarios, and proportional consequencesRequest P3 (si vous pouviez), complaint P3 (si vous ne procédez pas), negotiation P3 (si vous acceptiez)

These three systems interact constantly. A single sentence can contain all three:

Si vous acceptiez [si-clause: imparfait] de reconsidérer cette augmentation, je serais [conditional] très reconnaissant(e), car il est essentiel que nous puissions [subjunctive] parvenir à un accord.

Understanding each system individually AND how they combine is what separates B2 from B1.

2️⃣ SYSTEM 1: THE CONDITIONAL PRESENT — COMPLETE MASTERY

2A. What the Conditional Does in P3

The conditional present (le conditionnel présent) is the mood of possibility, politeness, and diplomacy. In P3, it serves four distinct functions:

FunctionWhat It CommunicatesExample
PolitenessSoftening a demand or requestJe souhaiterais obtenir un remboursement.
PossibilityPresenting a proposal as one option among othersCela permettrait de résoudre le problème.
HypothesisExpressing what would happen under certain conditionsJe me verrais contraint(e) de saisir le médiateur.
Intellectual cautionPresenting a claim as a reasoned position, not a dogmaIl conviendrait de privilégier l'éducation.

2B. How to Form the Conditional Present

The rule is simple: Take the future stem + add the imparfait endings (-ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient).

🔴 The most dangerous conditional error in TEF letters: Confusing the conditional (-ais) with the future (-ai) in the first person.

Future (je — certainty)Conditional (je — possibility/politeness)
je serai (I will be)je serais (I would be)
je ferai (I will do)je ferais (I would do)
j'aurai (I will have)j'aurais (I would have)
je pourrai (I will be able)je pourrais (I would be able)

The difference is a single letter: -ai (future) vs. -ais (conditional). In speech, the pronunciation may be similar. In writing, the distinction is critical. Using the future when the conditional is required makes your demand sound presumptuous (you are assuming the outcome rather than presenting it as a possibility).

⚠️ Mnemonic: If you are making a demand, expressing a wish, or presenting a possibility, you need the -ais ending. If you are stating a definite future fact, you need the -ai ending. When in doubt in P3, use -ais — the conditional is almost always the safer choice.

2D. The Past Conditional — Maximum Politeness

The past conditional (le conditionnel passé) adds another layer of distance and deference. It was introduced in Ch. 29 for cover letters and response letters, but it can appear in any P3 type where maximum politeness is needed.

Formation: Conditional of avoir or être + past participle

💡 The past conditional is a B2+/C1 marker. If you use it correctly once in your P3, the examiner notices. It signals awareness of the politeness gradient in French — you know that the conditional is polite, and the past conditional is even more polite. This is sociolinguistic sophistication.

⚠️ Do not overuse the past conditional. One instance per letter is sufficient. Using it in every sentence sounds unnatural and suggests you are performing a grammar exercise rather than writing a letter.

3️⃣ SYSTEM 2: THE SUBJUNCTIVE — COMPLETE MASTERY

3A. What the Subjunctive Does in P3

The subjunctive (le subjonctif) is the mood of subjectivity — it appears after expressions of necessity, doubt, emotion, will, and purpose. In P3, it serves three functions:

FunctionWhat It CommunicatesTrigger Examples
NecessityWhat must or should happenIl est essentiel que, il faudrait que, il est impératif que
PurposeWhat something is intended to achievePour que, afin que, de sorte que
Will/WishWhat you want or demandJe souhaite que, je demande que, j'exige que

3B. How to Form the Subjunctive Present

🔴 The espérer que trap: In Spanish and Italian, "hope that" takes the subjunctive. In French, espérer que takes the indicative: J'espère que vous pourrez m'accorder un entretien (NOT puissiez). This is one of the most common errors among multilingual candidates.

🔴 The après que trap: Après que takes the indicative (because the action has already happened), even though avant que takes the subjunctive. Many native French speakers incorrectly use the subjunctive after après que in speech, but in formal writing, the indicative is required:
✅ Après que j'ai contacté votre service...
❌ Après que j'aie contacté votre service...

3F. The Subjunctive Avoidance Strategy — When You Are Not Sure

If you are uncertain about the subjunctive form of a verb, you have two safe escape routes:

Escape Route 1: Replace the subjunctive clause with an infinitive construction

This works when the subject of both clauses is the same:

Subjunctive (Two Different Subjects)Infinitive (Same Subject)
Je souhaite que vous puissiez m'accorder un entretien.Je souhaite pouvoir vous rencontrer lors d'un entretien.
Il est important que je sois présent(e).Il est important d'être présent(e).
J'exige que vous me remboursiez.J'exige d'être remboursé(e).

The infinitive construction avoids the subjunctive entirely while maintaining the meaning and register.

Escape Route 2: Replace the subjunctive trigger with a non-trigger synonym

Subjunctive TriggerNon-Trigger ReplacementExample
Bien que + subjunctiveMême si + indicativeMême si je comprends vos motivations...
Il est essentiel que + subjunctiveIl est essentiel de + infinitiveIl est essentiel de prendre des mesures.
Pour que + subjunctiveAfin de + infinitive (same subject)Afin de résoudre ce problème...
Avant que + subjunctiveAvant de + infinitive (same subject)Avant de prendre une décision...
À condition que + subjunctiveÀ condition de + infinitive (same subject)À condition de respecter les délais...

💡 The strategic principle: It is always better to use a simpler structure correctly than a complex structure incorrectly. An infinitive construction that is grammatically perfect scores higher than a subjunctive construction with an error. The examiner rewards accuracy above ambition.

However: If you never use the subjunctive at all, the examiner notes the absence. The ideal strategy is to use the subjunctive once or twice in your letter with structures you are confident about (bien que + soit/soient, pour que + puisse/puissions, il est essentiel que + soit), and use infinitive alternatives for structures where you are less sure.

4️⃣ SYSTEM 3: THE SI-CLAUSE SYSTEM — COMPLETE MASTERY

4A. What Si-Clauses Do in P3

Si-clauses (les propositions conditionnelles) create hypothetical scenarios. In P3, they serve two primary functions:

FunctionStructureP3 Use
Firm conditionSi + present → futureComplaint P3: "If you don't do X, I will do Y"
Diplomatic conditionSi + imparfait → conditionalRequest/Suggestion/Negotiation P3: "If you could do X, I would be grateful"

4B. The Two Structures — Side by Side

Structure 1: Si + Present → Future (Factual/Firm)

Si-ClauseMain ClauseMeaning
Si vous ne procédez pas au remboursementje saisirai le médiateur."If you don't refund me, I will contact the mediator."
Si vous confirmez les conditionsje signerai le contrat."If you confirm the conditions, I will sign."
Si cette situation perdureje me verrai contraint(e) de..."If this situation continues, I will be compelled to..."

Tone: Direct, factual, consequence-oriented. Use in complaint P3 (Level 3–4) and firm response letters.

Structure 2: Si + Imparfait → Conditional (Diplomatic/Hypothetical)

Si-ClauseMain ClauseMeaning
Si vous pouviez m'accorder un entretienje vous en serais très reconnaissant(e)."If you could grant me an interview, I would be very grateful."
Si cette proposition retenait votre attentionje me rendrais disponible."If this proposal caught your attention, I would make myself available."
Si vous acceptiez de reconsidérerje serais disposé(e) à..."If you agreed to reconsider, I would be willing to..."
Si un tel aménagement était envisageableil permettrait de..."If such an arrangement were feasible, it would allow..."

Tone: Polite, hypothetical, diplomatic. Use in request P3, suggestion P3, cover letter P3, and negotiation P3.

4C. The Absolute Rule — What NEVER Follows Si

🔴 NEVER put the conditional or the future after si in the si-clause itself.

❌ WRONG✅ CORRECTRule
Si vous pourriez m'aider...Si vous pouviez m'aider...Si + imparfait (never conditional)
Si vous auriez le temps...Si vous aviez le temps...Si + imparfait (never conditional)
Si je serai disponible...Si je suis disponible...Si + present (never future)
Si cela conviendrait...Si cela convenait...Si + imparfait (never conditional)

This rule has NO exceptions in standard French. The conditional and the future NEVER appear in the si-clause — they appear only in the main clause (the result clause).

Mnemonic: "Les si n'aiment pas les -rais." (Si doesn't like the conditional endings.)

4D. The Third Structure — Si + Plus-que-parfait → Conditional Passé (Unrealized Past)

This structure expresses something that did not happen — a missed opportunity or an unrealized condition. It is rare in P3 but can appear in complaint letters referring to what should have happened:

Si-ClauseMain ClauseMeaning
Si votre service client avait répondu à mes appelscette réclamation n'aurait pas été nécessaire."If your customer service had answered my calls, this complaint would not have been necessary."
Si l'appareil avait été livré en bon étatje n'aurais pas eu à vous écrire."If the appliance had been delivered in good condition, I would not have had to write to you."

Tone: Reproachful but formal — "you had the chance to prevent this, and you didn't." Use sparingly in complaint P2 or P3 for maximum impact.

⚠️ Do not confuse the three structures:

2. DiplomaticImparfaitConditional presentPresent/Future (hypothetical)

5️⃣ HOW THE THREE SYSTEMS INTERACT — COMBINED STRUCTURES

The most sophisticated P3 sentences combine two or even three of these systems. Understanding how they interact is essential for B2/C1 writing.

Interaction 1: Si-Clause + Conditional (Standard Combination)

Si vous pouviez [imparfait] procéder au remboursement, je vous en serais [conditional] très reconnaissant(e).

This is the basic diplomatic request — covered extensively in Chapters 27 and 29.

Interaction 2: Si-Clause + Subjunctive (Via Embedded Clause)

Si cette proposition retenait [imparfait] votre attention, il serait [conditional] souhaitable que nous puissions [subjunctive] en discuter.

The si-clause triggers the conditional in the main clause. The conditional triggers the impersonal structure il serait souhaitable que, which in turn triggers the subjunctive. Three systems in one sentence.

Interaction 3: Conditional + Subjunctive (The Most Common P3 Combination)

Je souhaiterais [conditional] que vous puissiez [subjunctive] procéder au remplacement.

The conditional (souhaiterais) softens the demand. The que after souhaiter triggers the subjunctive (puissiez). This combination is the gold standard of diplomatic French: polite (conditional) AND grammatically accurate (subjunctive after souhaiter que).

More examples of Conditional + Subjunctive:

SentenceConditional ElementSubjunctive Element
Il conviendrait que les mesures soient prises rapidement.conviendrait (conditional of convenir)soient (subjunctive after il convient que)
Il faudrait que les pouvoirs publics investissent dans l'éducation.faudrait (conditional of falloir)investissent (subjunctive after il faut que)
Il serait souhaitable que nous puissions trouver un terrain d'entente.serait (conditional of être)puissions (subjunctive after il est souhaitable que)
Je souhaiterais que cette situation soit résolue.souhaiterais (conditional of souhaiter)soit (subjunctive after souhaiter que)
Il vaudrait mieux que vous preniez contact avec moi.vaudrait (conditional of valoir)preniez (subjunctive after il vaut mieux que)

💡 This combination — conditional + subjunctive — is the single most rewarded grammar structure in TEF P3. It demonstrates mastery of two advanced systems simultaneously. The examiner sees a candidate who can soften a demand (conditional) while maintaining grammatical accuracy (subjunctive). One correct instance of this combination in your P3 significantly boosts your grammar score.

Interaction 4: All Three Systems in One Paragraph

Here is a P3 paragraph that uses all three systems — conditional, subjunctive, and si-clause — in natural, integrated French:

Je souhaiterais [conditional] que cette situation puisse [subjunctive] être résolue dans les meilleurs délais. Si vous acceptiez [si-clause: imparfait] de procéder au remplacement de l'appareil, je vous en serais [conditional] très reconnaissant(e). Il serait [conditional] par ailleurs souhaitable que votre service client soit [subjunctive] informé de ce dysfonctionnement, afin que d'autres clients ne subissent [subjunctive] pas le même désagrément. Si toutefois ma demande devait [si-clause: imparfait] rester sans suite, je me verrais [conditional] contraint(e) de saisir le médiateur.

Grammar inventory:

  • Conditional: souhaiterais, serais, serait, me verrais (4 instances)
  • Subjunctive: puisse, soit, subissent (3 instances)
  • Si-clauses: si vous acceptiez... serais and si ma demande devait... me verrais (2 instances)
  • Three systems, perfectly integrated, in a single natural paragraph.

Exercise 3: Correct the Si-Clause Errors

Each sentence contains a si-clause error. Identify and correct it.

  1. ❌ Si vous pourriez me recevoir, je serais reconnaissant(e).
    ✅ Si vous pouviez me recevoir, je serais reconnaissant(e). (Conditional after si → imparfait)

  2. ❌ Si vous ne procéderiez pas au remboursement, je saisirai le médiateur.
    ✅ Si vous ne procédez pas au remboursement, je saisirai le médiateur. (Conditional after si → present; or: Si vous ne procédiez pas... je saisirais)

  3. ❌ Si ma candidature retiendrait votre attention, je me rendrais disponible.
    ✅ Si ma candidature retenait votre attention, je me rendrais disponible. (Conditional after si → imparfait)

  4. ❌ Si vous aurez le temps, pourriez-vous me rappeler ?
    ✅ Si vous avez le temps, pourriez-vous me rappeler ? (Future after si → present)

  5. ❌ Si cette solution conviendrait aux deux parties, nous pourrions signer.
    ✅ Si cette solution convenait aux deux parties, nous pourrions signer. (Conditional after si → imparfait)

9️⃣ B1 vs. B2 vs. C1 — THE SAME P3 CONTENT AT THREE GRAMMAR LEVELS

Content: You want a refund for a defective product. You will escalate if necessary. You are available to discuss.

B1 Grammar

Je veux un remboursement. Si vous ne me remboursez pas, je vais appeler un avocat. Vous pouvez m'appeler si vous voulez.

Grammar inventory:

  • Present indicative: je veux, vous ne remboursez pas, vous pouvez
  • Near future: je vais appeler
  • Si + present → near future (informal)
  • No conditional, no subjunctive
  • Score: Low — functional but unsophisticated

B2 Grammar

Je souhaiterais obtenir le remboursement intégral de la somme versée dans un délai de quinze jours. Si ma demande devait rester sans suite, je me verrais contraint(e) de saisir le médiateur de la consommation. Je reste néanmoins à votre disposition pour tout échange que vous jugeriez utile afin de résoudre ce litige à l'amiable.

Grammar inventory:

  • Conditional: souhaiterais, me verrais, jugeriez (3 instances)
  • Si-clause: si ma demande devait (imparfait) → me verrais (conditional)
  • Implicit subjunctive avoidance: afin de résoudre (infinitive instead of afin que nous puissions résoudre)
  • Relative clause: que vous jugeriez utile
  • Score: Solid B2 — conditional mastery demonstrated, diplomatic structures in place

C1 Grammar

Je vous saurais gré de bien vouloir procéder au remboursement intégral de ma commande, soit la somme de 349 euros, dans un délai de quinze jours à compter de la réception du présent courrier. Si, contre toute attente, ma demande devait rester sans suite, je me verrais dans l'obligation d'engager les démarches nécessaires auprès des instances compétentes — démarche que j'aurais préféré éviter. Il n'en demeure pas moins que je souhaiterais vivement que ce litige puisse être résolu de manière amiable et me tiens à votre entière disposition pour tout échange que vous jugeriez opportun à cette fin.

Grammar inventory:

  • Conditional: saurais, souhaiterais, jugeriez (3 instances)
  • Past conditional: j'aurais préféré éviter (1 instance — maximum politeness)
  • Subjunctive: puisse after souhaiterais que (1 instance — conditional + subjunctive combination)
  • Si-clause: si ma demande devait (imparfait) → me verrais (conditional)
  • Fixed expressions: je vous saurais gré de bien vouloir, il n'en demeure pas moins que, à cette fin
  • Relative clause: que vous jugeriez opportun
  • Appositive: soit la somme de 349 euros
  • Parenthetical: démarche que j'aurais préféré éviter — embedded regret using past conditional
  • Score: C1 — all three systems deployed naturally, past conditional for nuance, conditional + subjunctive combination, sophisticated fixed expressions

🔟 THE GRAMMAR SAFETY NET — WHAT TO DO WHEN YOU FORGET

Under exam pressure, you may forget a conjugation, a trigger rule, or a stem. Here is your emergency protocol:

Emergency 1: You Forget a Conditional Form

Problem: You cannot remember the conditional of pouvoir.
Solution: Use être en mesure de (present) instead:

Instead of: Je pourrais vous rencontrer...
Write: Je suis en mesure de vous rencontrer... or Il me serait possible de vous rencontrer...

Emergency 2: You Forget Whether a Trigger Takes the Subjunctive

Problem: You are not sure if il est important que takes the subjunctive.
Solution: Restructure with an infinitive:

Instead of: Il est important que les mesures soient prises...
Write: Il est important de prendre les mesures nécessaires...

Emergency 3: You Forget the Subjunctive of a Specific Verb

Problem: You need the subjunctive of résoudre and cannot remember it.
Solution: Use a synonym whose subjunctive you know:

Instead of: pour que ce problème soit résolu...
Write: pour que nous puissions trouver une solution... (puisse/puissions is the most useful subjunctive form — memorize it)

Emergency 4: You Cannot Remember Which Si-Structure to Use

Problem: You are unsure whether to use present or imparfait after si.
Solution: Ask yourself: "Am I being firm or diplomatic?"

Firm (complaint, warning) → Si + present → future
Diplomatic (request, proposal) → Si + imparfait → conditional
If still unsure → Use the imparfait → conditional structure. It is never wrong in tone (it is always polite), and it is the more common structure in TEF P3.

Emergency 5: You Have Written Something and Are Not Sure If It Is Correct

Problem: You wrote si vous pourriez and are now unsure.
Solution: During your editing time (Ch. 42), check every si-clause. If si is followed by a word ending in -rais/-rait/-rions/-riez/-raient, it is WRONG. Cross it out and replace with the imparfait form.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY — WHAT THIS CHAPTER TEACHES THAT NO OTHER CHAPTER COVERS

Chapter 31 is the only chapter that consolidates all three grammar systems of P3 — conditional, subjunctive, and si-clauses — into a single integrated reference, showing not just how each system works individually, but how they combine in the sentences that earn the highest scores.

The core principles:

The conditional is the dominant mood of P3 across all letter types. It softens demands (complaint), expresses wishes (request), presents possibilities (suggestion), projects confidence (cover letter), frames counter-proposals (response), and proposes societal changes (opinion). Mastering the conditional forms of 9 key irregular verbs (être, avoir, pouvoir, vouloir, savoir, voir, faire, devoir, falloir) gives you the tools to write any P3 at B2 level. The difference between -ai (future) and -ais (conditional) in the first person is the most consequential single letter in TEF writing.

The subjunctive is the grammar of necessity, purpose, and concession in P3. It appears after specific triggers — il est essentiel que, pour que, afin que, bien que, je souhaite que, à condition que — and nowhere else. The strategic approach is to use the subjunctive once or twice in your letter with forms you are confident about (soit/soient, puisse/puissions, fasse, comprenne), and use infinitive alternatives for everything else. One correct subjunctive is worth more than three incorrect ones.

The si-clause system has exactly two structures for P3: si + present → future (firm/factual) and si + imparfait → conditional (diplomatic/hypothetical). The absolute rule — NEVER put the conditional after si — has no exceptions. If you remember nothing else from this chapter, remember this rule: "Les si n'aiment pas les -rais."

The conditional + subjunctive combination is the gold standard of P3 grammar. Je souhaiterais que vous puissiez, il serait souhaitable que les mesures soient prises, il faudrait que les pouvoirs publics investissent — these sentences deploy two advanced grammar systems simultaneously, demonstrating both politeness (conditional) and grammatical accuracy (subjunctive). One correct instance of this combination in your P3 is the single most effective grammar move you can make.

The safety net exists for a reason. Under exam pressure, use it without shame. If you forget a conditional form, use être en mesure de. If you forget whether a trigger takes the subjunctive, restructure with an infinitive. If you are unsure about a si-clause, default to si + imparfait → conditional (it is always polite and almost always appropriate). A simpler structure used correctly always scores higher than a complex structure used incorrectly.

The grammar decision tree (Section 8) is your exam-day mental model. Before writing each P3 sentence, ask: "Am I demanding, requesting, proposing, expressing availability, or negotiating?" The answer determines which grammar system dominates. Then ask: "Am I being firm or diplomatic?" The answer determines which structure within that system to use. These two questions, asked in sequence, lead you to the correct grammar every time.
